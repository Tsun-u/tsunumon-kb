# C.2: Wave Model

> Theme C: Wave Behaviour | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Wave Properties
- **Wavelength** ($\lambda$): distance between consecutive identical points on a wave
- **Frequency** ($f$): number of complete oscillations per second (Hz)
- **Period** ($T$): time for one complete oscillation; $T = 1/f$
- **Amplitude** ($A$): maximum displacement from equilibrium
- **Wave speed**: $v = f\lambda$

### Types of Waves
- **Transverse waves**: oscillation perpendicular to direction of energy transfer
  - Examples: electromagnetic waves, waves on a string, water surface waves
- **Longitudinal waves**: oscillation parallel to direction of energy transfer
  - Examples: sound waves, compression waves in springs
  - Compressions and rarefactions

### Wave Equation
- $v = f\lambda$
- Speed depends on the medium, not on frequency or amplitude (for mechanical waves)

### Electromagnetic Spectrum
- All EM waves travel at $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in vacuum
- Spectrum (increasing frequency): radio, microwave, infrared, visible, ultraviolet, X-ray, gamma
- EM waves are transverse and do not require a medium

### Wave Intensity
- **Intensity** ($I$): power per unit area; $I = \frac{P}{A}$
- SI unit: W/m²
- Intensity is proportional to amplitude squared: $I \propto A^2$
- For a point source: $I = \frac{P}{4\pi r^2}$ (inverse square law)

### Superposition
- **Principle of superposition**: when waves meet, the resultant displacement is the sum of individual displacements
- **Constructive interference**: waves in phase → larger amplitude
- **Destructive interference**: waves out of phase → smaller or zero amplitude

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $v = f\lambda$ | Wave equation |
| $T = 1/f$ | Period-frequency relation |
| $I = P/A$ | Wave intensity |
| $I \propto A^2$ | Intensity-amplitude relationship |
| $I = P/(4\pi r^2)$ | Inverse square law |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Waves transport matter"** -- Waves transfer energy and information, not matter. Particles oscillate about their equilibrium positions.
2. **"Louder sounds travel faster"** -- Amplitude affects loudness (intensity), not speed. Speed depends on the medium.
3. **"All waves require a medium"** -- Electromagnetic waves can travel through a vacuum.
4. **"Higher frequency means higher speed"** -- For waves in the same medium, speed is constant; higher frequency means shorter wavelength.
