## Key Concepts

### Wave Properties
- **Wavelength** ($\lambda$): the spatial length of one complete wave cycle, measured between two successive points of identical phase
- **Frequency** ($f$): how many complete cycles pass a fixed point each second, measured in Hz
- **Period** ($T$): the time required to complete exactly one full oscillation cycle; $T = 1/f$
- **Amplitude** ($A$): the peak displacement of the medium from its undisturbed position
- **Wave speed**: $v = f\lambda$

### Types of Waves
- **Transverse waves**: the oscillating medium moves at right angles to the direction in which energy travels
  - Examples: electromagnetic waves, waves on a string, water surface waves
- **Longitudinal waves**: the oscillating medium moves along the same axis as the energy flow
  - Examples: sound waves, compression waves in springs
  - Compressions and rarefactions

### Wave Equation
- $v = f\lambda$
- For mechanical waves, speed is set by the properties of the medium and does not change with frequency or amplitude

### Electromagnetic Spectrum
- All EM waves travel at $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in vacuum
- Spectrum (increasing frequency): radio, microwave, infrared, visible, ultraviolet, X-ray, gamma
- EM waves are transverse and propagate through empty space without needing any material medium

### Wave Intensity
- **Intensity** ($I$): the rate of energy flow per unit cross-sectional area; $I = \frac{P}{A}$
- SI unit: W/m²
- Intensity scales with the square of the amplitude: $I \propto A^2$
- For a point source radiating uniformly in all directions: $I = \frac{P}{4\pi r^2}$ (inverse square law)

### Superposition
- **Principle of superposition**: at any point where two or more waves overlap, the net displacement of the medium equals the algebraic sum of the individual wave displacements at that moment
- **Constructive interference**: overlapping waves that are in phase combine to produce a larger resultant amplitude
- **Destructive interference**: overlapping waves that are out of phase partially or completely cancel each other

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $v = f\lambda$ | Wave equation |
| $T = 1/f$ | Period-frequency relation |
| $I = P/A$ | Wave intensity |
| $I \propto A^2$ | Intensity-amplitude relationship |
| $I = P/(4\pi r^2)$ | Inverse square law |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Waves transport matter"** -- Waves transfer energy and information, not matter. Particles oscillate about their equilibrium positions.
2. **"Louder sounds travel faster"** -- Amplitude affects loudness (intensity), not speed. Speed depends on the medium.
3. **"All waves require a medium"** -- Electromagnetic waves can travel through a vacuum.
4. **"Higher frequency means higher speed"** -- For waves in the same medium, speed is constant; higher frequency means shorter wavelength.
