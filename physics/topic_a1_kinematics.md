## Key Concepts

### Displacement, Velocity, and Acceleration
- **Displacement** ($s$): the net positional shift of an object from its starting point to its endpoint, treated as a vector quantity
- **Velocity** ($v$): how quickly displacement accumulates over time; a vector pointing in the direction of motion
  - Average velocity: $\bar{v} = \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t}$
  - Instantaneous velocity: $v = \frac{ds}{dt}$
- **Acceleration** ($a$): the rate at which velocity changes with time; a vector that can point in any direction relative to motion
  - $a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$ or $a = \frac{dv}{dt}$
- Distance is a scalar total path length; displacement is a vector net change in position. Speed is scalar; velocity is the vector counterpart.

### Uniformly Accelerated Motion
- Kinematic equations (SUVAT, constant acceleration):

$$v = u + at$$

$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$

$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

$$s = \frac{(u + v)}{2} \cdot t$$

### Graphs of Motion
- Position-time graph: the gradient at any point gives the instantaneous velocity
- Velocity-time graph: the gradient gives acceleration; the enclosed area gives displacement
- Acceleration-time graph: the enclosed area gives the total change in velocity

### Projectile Motion
- Horizontal and vertical motion are fully independent of each other
- Horizontal: $a_x = 0$, so the horizontal velocity component stays fixed throughout flight
- Vertical: $a_y = g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ directed downward at all times
- Range, maximum height, and time of flight follow from applying the SUVAT equations to each component separately
- Air resistance reduces range and maximum height; the trajectory becomes asymmetric (qualitative treatment)

### Relative Motion
- Relative velocity: $\vec{v}_{AB} = \vec{v}_A - \vec{v}_B$
- The measured velocity of an object depends on the chosen reference frame; Galilean transformations describe how coordinates and velocities relate between inertial frames (connects to A.5 at HL)

## Key Equations

| Equation | Variables |
|----------|----------|
| $v = u + at$ | $v$: final velocity, $u$: initial velocity, $a$: acceleration, $t$: time |
| $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ | $s$: displacement |
| $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$ | Links velocity to displacement without time |
| $s = \frac{(u+v)}{2}t$ | Average velocity method |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"An object at rest has no acceleration"** -- An object can have zero velocity but non-zero acceleration (e.g., at the peak of projectile motion).
2. **"Heavier objects fall faster"** -- In a vacuum, all objects experience the same gravitational acceleration.
3. **"A projectile's acceleration changes during flight"** -- Without air resistance, acceleration is constant at $g$ downward throughout the flight.
4. **"Velocity and acceleration must have the same sign"** -- An object can slow down (velocity and acceleration in opposite directions).
