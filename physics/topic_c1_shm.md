# C.1: Simple Harmonic Motion

> Theme C: Wave Behaviour | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Definition of SHM
- **Simple harmonic motion**: oscillation where restoring force is proportional to displacement and directed toward equilibrium
- $a = -\omega^2 x$ (defining equation of SHM)
- Restoring force: $F = -kx$ (spring) or $F = -mg\sin\theta \approx -mg\theta$ (small-angle pendulum)

### SHM Equations
- Displacement: $x = x_0 \sin(\omega t)$ or $x = x_0 \cos(\omega t)$
- Velocity: $v = \omega x_0 \cos(\omega t)$ or $v = \pm\omega\sqrt{x_0^2 - x^2}$
- Acceleration: $a = -\omega^2 x_0 \sin(\omega t) = -\omega^2 x$
- $x_0$: amplitude (maximum displacement)
- $\omega = 2\pi f = \frac{2\pi}{T}$: angular frequency

### Period of Oscillation
- **Mass-spring system**: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$
  - Independent of amplitude
- **Simple pendulum**: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$
  - Independent of mass and amplitude (for small angles)

### Energy in SHM
- Total energy = kinetic energy + potential energy = constant (no damping)
- $E_k = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(x_0^2 - x^2)$
- $E_p = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x^2$
- $E_{total} = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x_0^2 = \frac{1}{2}kx_0^2$
- At equilibrium: all kinetic; at extremes: all potential

### Damping
- **Underdamped**: oscillations with gradually decreasing amplitude
- **Critically damped**: returns to equilibrium as fast as possible without oscillation
- **Overdamped**: returns to equilibrium slowly without oscillation
- Natural frequency vs. damped frequency

### Forced Oscillations and Resonance
- **Driving frequency**: frequency of external periodic force
- **Resonance**: maximum amplitude when driving frequency equals natural frequency
- Sharpness of resonance peak depends on damping
- Applications: musical instruments, radio tuning, earthquake damage
- Destructive resonance: Tacoma Narrows Bridge, wine glass shattering

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"SHM amplitude affects the period"** -- For ideal SHM, period is independent of amplitude.
2. **"Resonance means destruction"** -- Resonance is a useful phenomenon (musical instruments, MRI); it is only destructive when amplitude becomes too large.
3. **"SHM requires a spring"** -- Any system with a restoring force proportional to displacement exhibits SHM.
4. **"Damping stops oscillation immediately"** -- Underdamped systems oscillate many times before stopping.
