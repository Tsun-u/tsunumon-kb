## Key Concepts

### First Law of Thermodynamics
- $\Delta U = Q - W$ (or $Q = \Delta U + W$)
  - $\Delta U$: change in internal energy
  - $Q$: heat added to the system
  - $W$: work done by the system
- Conservation of energy applied to thermal processes
- Work done by a gas: $W = p\Delta V$ (at constant pressure)
- Work = area under p-V curve

### Thermodynamic Processes
- **Isothermal** ($\Delta T = 0$): $\Delta U = 0$, so $Q = W$; occurs slowly, in thermal contact
- **Isobaric** ($\Delta p = 0$): $W = p\Delta V$; $Q = \Delta U + p\Delta V$
- **Isovolumetric / Isochoric** ($\Delta V = 0$): $W = 0$; $Q = \Delta U$
- **Adiabatic** ($Q = 0$): $\Delta U = -W$; occurs rapidly, thermally insulated

### p-V Diagrams
- Each process has a characteristic shape on a p-V diagram
- Clockwise cycle: heat engine (net work output)
- Counterclockwise cycle: heat pump / refrigerator (net work input)
- Net work per cycle = area enclosed by the cycle

### Second Law of Thermodynamics
- Heat cannot spontaneously flow from cold to hot
- Entropy of an isolated system tends to increase
- **Entropy** ($S$): measure of disorder; $\Delta S = \frac{Q}{T}$ (for reversible processes)
- All real processes are irreversible; entropy always increases overall

### Heat Engines and Efficiency
- Heat engine: converts thermal energy to mechanical work
- Efficiency: $\eta = \frac{W}{Q_H} = 1 - \frac{Q_C}{Q_H}$
- **Carnot efficiency** (maximum possible): $\eta_{Carnot} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$ (temperatures in K)
- No real engine can exceed Carnot efficiency

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $Q = \Delta U + W$ | First law of thermodynamics |
| $W = p\Delta V$ | Work at constant pressure |
| $\eta = 1 - \frac{Q_C}{Q_H}$ | Engine efficiency |
| $\eta_{Carnot} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$ | Maximum (Carnot) efficiency |
| $\Delta S = \frac{Q}{T}$ | Entropy change (reversible) |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Entropy means chaos"** -- Entropy is a precise thermodynamic quantity measuring the number of microstates; "disorder" is an oversimplification.
2. **"Perpetual motion machines are theoretically possible"** -- The second law prohibits 100% efficient conversion of heat to work.
3. **"Adiabatic means isothermal"** -- Adiabatic means no heat transfer; the temperature can and does change.
4. **"The Carnot cycle is achievable"** -- Carnot efficiency is an ideal upper limit; real engines always have lower efficiency.
