## Key Concepts

### Photoelectric Effect
- Light hitting a metal surface can eject electrons
- Observations that classical wave theory cannot explain:
  - Below threshold frequency ($f_0$), no electrons emitted regardless of intensity
  - Kinetic energy of emitted electrons depends on frequency, not intensity
  - Emission is instantaneous (no time delay)
- **Einstein's explanation**: light consists of photons with energy $E = hf$
  - $E_k^{max} = hf - \phi$ (photoelectric equation)
  - $\phi = hf_0$: work function (minimum energy to release an electron)
  - Stopping potential: $eV_s = E_k^{max}$

### Wave-Particle Duality
- Light exhibits both wave and particle properties
- **de Broglie hypothesis**: all matter has wave properties
  - de Broglie wavelength: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$
- Evidence: electron diffraction patterns
- Wave behavior significant when $\lambda$ is comparable to the size of obstacles/slits

### Uncertainty Principle
- **Heisenberg uncertainty principle**:
  - $\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$
  - Position and momentum cannot both be known precisely simultaneously
  - $\Delta E \cdot \Delta t \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$
- Not a limitation of measurement technology but a fundamental property of nature

### Quantum Tunneling
- Particles can pass through potential energy barriers that are classically forbidden
- Probability decreases exponentially with barrier width and height
- Applications: nuclear fusion in stars, scanning tunneling microscope, tunnel diodes

### Matter Waves and the Bohr Model
- Standing wave condition: $2\pi r = n\lambda$ (electrons as standing waves around the nucleus)
- Quantization of angular momentum: $L = n\frac{h}{2\pi}$
- Explains why only certain energy levels are allowed

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $E = hf$ | Photon energy |
| $E_k^{max} = hf - \phi$ | Photoelectric equation |
| $\lambda = h/p = h/(mv)$ | de Broglie wavelength |
| $\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq h/(4\pi)$ | Heisenberg uncertainty (position-momentum) |
| $\phi = hf_0$ | Work function |
| $eV_s = hf - \phi$ | Stopping potential |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"The photoelectric effect proves light is a particle"** -- It shows light has particle-like properties; interference/diffraction show wave-like properties. Light has both.
2. **"The uncertainty principle is about measurement error"** -- It is a fundamental limit on what can be known, not a technological limitation.
3. **"Only small particles have wave properties"** -- All matter has a de Broglie wavelength, but for macroscopic objects it is immeasurably small.
4. **"Quantum tunneling violates conservation of energy"** -- Energy is conserved; the particle has the same energy before and after tunneling.
