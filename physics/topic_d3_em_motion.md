# D.3: Motion in Electromagnetic Fields

> Theme D: Fields | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Charged Particles in Electric Fields
- In a uniform field ($E = V/d$), a charged particle experiences constant force: $F = qE$
- Acceleration: $a = \frac{qE}{m}$
- Motion analogous to projectile motion:
  - If entering perpendicular to field: parabolic path
  - If entering parallel to field: linear acceleration/deceleration
- Energy gained: $W = qV$ (charge accelerated through potential difference $V$)
- $\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = qV$ → speed after acceleration: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$

### Charged Particles in Magnetic Fields
- Force: $F = qvB\sin\theta$ (perpendicular to both $v$ and $B$)
- When $v \perp B$: circular motion with radius $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$
- Period of circular motion: $T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB}$ (independent of speed)
- When $v$ has component parallel to $B$: helical (spiral) path

### Applications
- **Cathode ray tube (CRT)**: electrons accelerated and deflected by electric/magnetic fields
- **Mass spectrometer**: separates ions by mass-to-charge ratio
  - Ions accelerated through voltage, then deflected by magnetic field
  - $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$ → different masses follow different radii
  - $\frac{m}{q} = \frac{B^2 r^2}{2V}$ (after acceleration through voltage $V$)
- **Cyclotron**: charged particles accelerated in spiral path using alternating electric field and magnetic field
- **Velocity selector**: crossed electric and magnetic fields; only particles with $v = E/B$ pass through

### Electron Volt
- $1 \text{ eV} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ J
- Convenient unit for particle physics and atomic physics
- Kinetic energy of electron accelerated through 1 V = 1 eV

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $F = qE$ | Force on charge in electric field |
| $W = qV$ | Energy gained through potential difference |
| $r = mv/(qB)$ | Radius in magnetic field |
| $T = 2\pi m/(qB)$ | Period of circular motion in B field |
| $v = E/B$ | Velocity selector condition |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Charged particles always spiral in magnetic fields"** -- Circular motion occurs only when velocity is perpendicular to the field; if parallel, the particle is unaffected.
2. **"Electric and magnetic fields affect particles the same way"** -- Electric fields change speed; magnetic fields change direction (at constant speed).
3. **"The cyclotron period depends on speed"** -- Remarkably, $T = 2\pi m/(qB)$ is independent of speed (non-relativistic case).
4. **"Mass spectrometers measure mass directly"** -- They measure mass-to-charge ratio ($m/q$).
