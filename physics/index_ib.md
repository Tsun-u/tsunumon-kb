# IB® Physics (SL/HL) -- Course Framework

> Aligned with the IB Diploma Programme Physics guide, first assessment 2025.
> Last updated: 2026-03-13

## Course Structure

The IB Physics syllabus is organized into **five themes** containing **24 topics**. SL students cover 19 topics (some partially); HL students cover all 24 topics with additional depth.

### Five Themes

| Code | Theme | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **A** | Space, Time and Motion | Mechanics, kinematics, dynamics, energy, relativity |
| **B** | The Particulate Nature of Matter | Thermal physics, gas laws, circuits |
| **C** | Wave Behaviour | SHM, waves, wave phenomena, standing waves, Doppler |
| **D** | Fields | Gravitational, electric, magnetic fields and induction |
| **E** | Nuclear and Quantum Physics | Atomic structure, radioactivity, fission, fusion, quantum |

## Inquiry-Based Approach

The course develops three interconnected investigative skills:
1. **Formulating Investigations** -- developing research questions, stating hypotheses, and identifying controlled and measured variables
2. **Data Collection and Analysis** -- selecting appropriate methods, recording measurements accurately, and processing results to reveal patterns
3. **Interpretation and Reflection** -- drawing reasoned conclusions, assessing sources of error, and communicating findings clearly

### Investigative Tools
- **Lab Techniques** -- hands-on skills including equipment handling and safety protocols
- **Technology** -- data loggers, sensors, simulation software, and spreadsheet tools
- **Mathematical Methods** -- graph construction, propagation of uncertainty, and quantitative modeling

## Key Differences from AP Physics

- IB Physics is **one unified course** (SL/HL) vs. AP's separate Physics 1, Physics 2, Physics C courses
- IB HL covers **special relativity** (A.5) and **quantum physics** (E.2); AP Physics 1 does not
- IB includes **thermodynamics** (B.4, HL) and **greenhouse effect** (B.2); AP Physics 1 has no thermal physics
- IB covers **nuclear physics** (Theme E) extensively; AP Physics 1 does not
- AP Physics 1 is algebra-based; IB Physics uses calculus concepts at HL
- IB requires a mandatory **Internal Assessment** (scientific investigation)
- IB emphasizes **linking questions** between themes; AP uses cross-cutting Science Practices

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always specify SL vs. HL content boundaries clearly
- Use topic codes (e.g., A.2, C.3) when referencing content
- IB provides a **data booklet** during exams; students should know what is provided vs. what must be memorized
- Emphasize conceptual understanding and real-world applications (IB assessment style)
- HL content builds on SL; do not teach HL-only topics in isolation
