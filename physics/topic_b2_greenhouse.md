## Key Concepts

### Solar Radiation and Earth's Energy Balance
- Sun emits radiation with peak wavelength in visible spectrum (~500 nm)
- Earth absorbs solar radiation and re-emits as infrared (longer wavelength)
- Energy balance: incoming solar radiation = outgoing terrestrial radiation (at equilibrium)
- **Albedo**: fraction of incoming radiation reflected by a surface
  - $\alpha = \frac{\text{total reflected power}}{\text{total incident power}}$
  - Ice/snow: high albedo (~0.8--0.9); forests: low albedo (~0.1--0.2)

### The Greenhouse Effect
- **Natural greenhouse effect**: essential for life; keeps Earth ~33°C warmer than without atmosphere
- Mechanism:
  1. Short-wavelength solar radiation passes through the atmosphere
  2. Earth's surface absorbs radiation and warms
  3. Earth re-emits long-wavelength infrared radiation
  4. Greenhouse gases absorb and re-emit infrared in all directions
  5. Some infrared is directed back toward the surface → additional warming
- **Greenhouse gases**: CO2, H2O, CH4, N2O, CFCs
  - Molecular vibrations absorb specific infrared wavelengths

### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect
- Human activities increase greenhouse gas concentrations:
  - CO2: fossil fuel combustion, deforestation
  - CH4: agriculture (livestock, rice paddies), landfills
  - N2O: fertilizers, industrial processes
- Global temperature increase correlates with rising CO2 levels
- Consequences: melting ice, rising sea levels, extreme weather, ecosystem disruption

### Energy Balance Calculations
- Solar constant: $S \approx 1361$ W/m² (power per unit area at Earth's distance)
- Average power absorbed: $\frac{S(1-\alpha)\pi r^2}{4\pi r^2} = \frac{S(1-\alpha)}{4}$
- Black body temperature of Earth (without atmosphere): ~255 K (−18°C)
- Actual average surface temperature: ~288 K (+15°C) → greenhouse effect adds ~33 K

| Equation | Description |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"The greenhouse effect is bad"** -- The natural greenhouse effect is essential for life; the enhanced (human-caused) effect is the concern.
2. **"The ozone hole causes global warming"** -- Ozone depletion and global warming are separate issues, though both involve atmospheric chemistry.
3. **"CO2 is the only greenhouse gas"** -- Water vapor is actually the most abundant greenhouse gas; methane is also significant.
4. **"Global warming means every place gets hotter"** -- Climate change involves complex patterns including some areas experiencing more extreme cold events.
