# D.1: Gravitational Fields

> Theme D: Fields | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Gravitational Field Strength
- **Gravitational field**: the region of space around a massive object where another mass would feel an attractive pull
- Field strength: $g = \frac{F}{m}$ (force per unit mass); SI unit: N/kg or m/s²
- Near Earth's surface: $g \approx 9.81$ m/s² (approximately uniform)
- Direction: always points toward the center of the mass producing the field

### Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation
- $F = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
- $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}$ N m² kg⁻²
- Gravitational field of a point mass: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- Inverse square law: doubling the distance reduces field strength to one quarter

### Gravitational Field Lines
- Arrows point radially inward, converging on the mass
- More densely packed lines indicate a stronger gravitational field
- For a uniform field (e.g., near a planetary surface): lines are parallel, evenly spaced, and directed downward

### Orbital Motion
- Gravitational force provides centripetal force for orbits:
  - $\frac{GMm}{r^2} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
  - Orbital speed: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
  - Orbital period: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}}$
- **Kepler's third law**: $T^2 \propto r^3$

### Gravitational Potential Energy (HL extension)
- $E_p = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ (negative; zero at infinity)
- Near surface approximation: $\Delta E_p = mgh$ (for small heights)
- **Escape velocity**: $v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$

### Gravitational Potential (HL)
- $V_g = -\frac{GM}{r}$ (potential per unit mass; scalar)
- $g = -\frac{dV_g}{dr}$ (field strength is negative gradient of potential)

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $F = GMm/r^2$ | Newton's law of gravitation |
| $g = GM/r^2$ | Gravitational field strength |
| $v = \sqrt{GM/r}$ | Orbital speed |
| $T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}$ | Kepler's third law |
| $E_p = -GMm/r$ | Gravitational PE (HL) |
| $v_{esc} = \sqrt{2GM/r}$ | Escape velocity (HL) |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"There is no gravity in space"** -- Gravity extends infinitely; astronauts in orbit are in free fall, not zero gravity.
2. **"Satellites need engines to stay in orbit"** -- Orbits are maintained by gravitational force alone; engines are only needed for orbit changes.
3. **"Gravitational PE is always positive"** -- With the convention of zero at infinity, gravitational PE is always negative.
4. **"Heavier satellites orbit faster"** -- Orbital speed is independent of satellite mass; it depends only on the central mass and orbital radius.
