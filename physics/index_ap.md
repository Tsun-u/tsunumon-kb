# AP Physics 1: Algebra-Based -- Course Framework

> Based on the College Board Course and Exam Description (CED), effective Fall 2024.
> Last updated: 2026-03-12

## Important Note on Course Structure

As of the 2024-25 school year, AP Physics 1 has been reorganized into **8 units** (previously 7).
Key changes:
- Circular motion moved into Unit 2 (Force and Translational Dynamics)
- Rotational motion split into Unit 5 (Torque and Rotational Dynamics) and Unit 6 (Energy and Momentum of Rotating Systems)
- Simple Harmonic Motion is now Unit 7 (Oscillations)
- **Unit 8 (Fluids) is entirely new**

## Science Practices

Students develop the following skills throughout the course:

1. **Modeling** -- Creating and using representations and models
2. **Mathematical Routines** -- Using mathematics appropriately
3. **Scientific Questioning** -- Engaging in scientific questioning and argumentation
4. **Experimental Methods** -- Planning and implementing data collection strategies
5. **Data Analysis** -- Performing data analysis and evaluation of evidence
6. **Argumentation** -- Working with scientific explanations and theories

## Exam Format (2025)

| Section | Question Type | Count | Time | Weight |
|---------|--------------|-------|------|--------|
| I | Multiple Choice | 40 | 80 min | 50% |
| II | Free Response | 4 | 100 min | 50% |

### Free Response Question Types
- **QQT** (Qualitative/Quantitative Translation): 1 question, 25 min
- **Short Answer**: 2 questions, 18 min each
- **Paragraph Argument Short Answer**: 1 question, 25 min
