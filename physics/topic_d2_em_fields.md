# D.2: Electric and Magnetic Fields

> Theme D: Fields | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Electric Charge
- Two types: positive and negative; like charges repel, unlike attract
- Elementary charge: $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
- Charge is quantized and conserved

### Coulomb's Law
- $F = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$ where $k = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} = 8.99 \times 10^9$ N m² C⁻²
- Inverse square law; analogous to gravitational force

### Electric Field
- $\vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q}$ (force per unit positive charge)
- Field of a point charge: $E = k\frac{Q}{r^2}$
- **Uniform field** between parallel plates: $E = \frac{V}{d}$
- Field lines: point from positive to negative; density indicates strength

### Electric Potential (HL extension)
- $V = k\frac{Q}{r}$ (potential due to point charge)
- $V = \frac{W}{q}$ (work per unit charge from infinity)
- Equipotential surfaces: perpendicular to field lines; no work done moving along them
- $E = -\frac{dV}{dr}$

### Electric Potential Energy
- $E_p = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$ (two point charges)
- Work done moving charge in uniform field: $W = qEd$

### Magnetic Fields
- Created by moving charges (currents)
- **Magnetic field** ($B$): measured in tesla (T)
- Field around a long straight wire: concentric circles (right-hand rule)
- Field of a solenoid: uniform inside, similar to bar magnet outside

### Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor
- $F = BIL\sin\theta$ (force on wire of length $L$ carrying current $I$)
- Direction: use left-hand rule (or right-hand rule depending on convention)

### Force on a Moving Charge
- $F = qvB\sin\theta$
- Force is perpendicular to both velocity and field → circular motion
- Radius of circular path: $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $F = kq_1q_2/r^2$ | Coulomb's law |
| $E = kQ/r^2$ | Electric field (point charge) |
| $E = V/d$ | Uniform electric field |
| $V = kQ/r$ | Electric potential (HL) |
| $F = BIL\sin\theta$ | Force on current-carrying wire |
| $F = qvB\sin\theta$ | Force on moving charge |
| $r = mv/(qB)$ | Radius of circular motion in B field |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Electric field lines can cross"** -- Field lines never cross; at each point there is a single field direction.
2. **"Magnetic force does work"** -- Magnetic force is always perpendicular to velocity, so it does no work; it changes direction, not speed.
3. **"Electric potential is the same as electric field"** -- Potential is a scalar (energy per charge); field is a vector (force per charge).
4. **"Magnetic poles exist independently"** -- No magnetic monopoles have been found; magnetic poles always come in N-S pairs.
