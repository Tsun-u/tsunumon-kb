# E.3: Radioactive Decay

> Theme E: Nuclear and Quantum Physics | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Types of Radioactive Decay
- **Alpha decay** ($\alpha$): nucleus emits $^4_2He$
  - $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + ^4_2He$
  - High ionizing power, low penetrating power (stopped by paper/skin)
- **Beta-minus decay** ($\beta^-$): neutron → proton + electron + antineutrino
  - $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1}Y + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$
  - Moderate ionizing and penetrating power (stopped by aluminum)
- **Beta-plus decay** ($\beta^+$): proton → neutron + positron + neutrino
  - $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1}Y + ^0_{+1}e + \nu_e$
- **Gamma decay** ($\gamma$): nucleus releases excess energy as a photon
  - No change in $A$ or $Z$
  - Low ionizing power, high penetrating power (attenuated by thick lead/concrete)

### Properties of Radiation

| Property | Alpha ($\alpha$) | Beta ($\beta$) | Gamma ($\gamma$) |
|----------|---------|---------|----------|
| Particle | $^4_2He$ | $^0_{-1}e$ (or $^0_{+1}e$) | photon |
| Charge | +2 | -1 (or +1) | 0 |
| Mass | 4 u | ~0 | 0 |
| Ionizing power | High | Medium | Low |
| Penetrating power | Low | Medium | High |
| Stopped by | Paper/skin | Aluminum (~mm) | Thick lead/concrete |

### Half-Life
- **Half-life** ($t_{1/2}$): time for half the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay
- Radioactive decay is random and spontaneous
- $N = N_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/t_{1/2}}$
- $N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$ where $\lambda$ = decay constant
- $\lambda = \frac{\ln 2}{t_{1/2}}$
- **Activity** ($A$): number of decays per second; $A = \lambda N$; SI unit: becquerel (Bq)

### Nuclear Stability
- Stable nuclei have balanced ratio of protons to neutrons
- Light nuclei: $N/Z \approx 1$; heavier nuclei need more neutrons ($N/Z > 1$)
- Nuclei above stability line: too many neutrons → $\beta^-$ decay
- Nuclei below stability line: too many protons → $\beta^+$ decay or electron capture
- Very heavy nuclei ($Z > 82$): tend to undergo $\alpha$ decay

### Background Radiation
- Natural sources: cosmic rays, rocks (radon), food (K-40, C-14)
- Artificial sources: medical procedures, nuclear testing, nuclear power
- Must be accounted for in measurements

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $N = N_0(1/2)^{t/t_{1/2}}$ | Exponential decay (half-life form) |
| $N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$ | Exponential decay (decay constant form) |
| $\lambda = \ln 2 / t_{1/2}$ | Decay constant and half-life relation |
| $A = \lambda N$ | Activity |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Radioactive decay can be sped up or slowed down"** -- Radioactive decay is unaffected by temperature, pressure, or chemical changes.
2. **"After two half-lives, all nuclei have decayed"** -- After two half-lives, 25% remain; after three, 12.5%, and so on.
3. **"Radiation always means nuclear radiation"** -- Radiation includes all EM waves; nuclear radiation is a specific subset.
4. **"All radiation is dangerous"** -- Risk depends on type, energy, exposure time, and distance. Alpha is only dangerous if ingested/inhaled.
