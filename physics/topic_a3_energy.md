# A.3: Work, Energy and Power

> Theme A: Space, Time and Motion | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Work
- Work done by a constant force: $W = Fs\cos\theta$
- Work is a scalar quantity; SI unit: joule (J)
- When the applied force varies, the total work equals the area under the force-displacement graph
- Work is positive when the force component aligns with the direction of motion; it is negative when the force opposes motion

### Kinetic Energy
- $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
- Work-kinetic energy theorem: $W_{net} = \Delta E_k$ — the net work done on an object equals the resulting change in its kinetic energy

### Potential Energy
- **Gravitational PE**: $E_p = mgh$ — energy stored by virtue of an object's height in a uniform gravitational field near Earth's surface
- **Elastic PE**: $E_p = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ — energy stored in a spring deformed by extension or compression $x$, valid as long as Hooke's law holds
- Potential energy is energy held in a system because of the arrangement or position of its parts

### Conservation of Energy
- The total energy of an isolated system remains fixed; it never disappears or appears from nothing
- Energy shifts between different forms—kinetic, potential, thermal, and others—but the total is unchanged
- $E_k + E_p = \text{constant}$ (in absence of non-conservative forces)
- When friction is present: $E_k + E_p + Q = \text{constant}$ (Q = thermal energy)

### Power
- Power measures how rapidly work is done or energy is transferred to a system
- $P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{\Delta E}{t}$
- $P = Fv$ — equivalently, power equals the product of the applied force and the object's speed
- SI unit: watt (W) = J/s

### Efficiency
- $\eta = \frac{\text{useful energy output}}{\text{total energy input}} \times 100\%$
- $\eta = \frac{\text{useful power output}}{\text{total power input}} \times 100\%$
- Every real-world machine loses some input energy as waste heat or sound; a 100% efficient machine cannot exist

|----------|-------------|
| $W = Fs\cos\theta$ | Work done by constant force |

| $E_p = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ | Elastic potential energy |
| $P = \frac{W}{t} = Fv$ | Power |
| $\eta = \frac{E_{useful}}{E_{total}} \times 100\%$ | Efficiency |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Energy is consumed or used up"** -- Energy is transferred or transformed, never created or destroyed.
2. **"Work requires movement"** -- If there is no displacement, no work is done regardless of the force applied.
3. **"A force perpendicular to motion does work"** -- No; $W = Fs\cos 90° = 0$. Centripetal force does no work.
4. **"Power and energy are the same"** -- Power is the rate of energy transfer; a powerful engine transfers energy faster, not necessarily more.
