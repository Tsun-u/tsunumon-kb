# AP® Physics 2: Algebra-Based -- Course Framework

> Aligned with the AP Physics 2 course framework, Fall 2024 edition.
> Last updated: 2026-06-10

## Course Overview

AP Physics 2 is the algebra-based follow-on to AP Physics 1, covering material typical of a second-semester introductory college physics course. Unit numbering continues from AP Physics 1, beginning at Unit 9.

Major topic areas:
- **Thermodynamics** (Unit 9): kinetic theory, ideal gas behavior, laws of thermodynamics, entropy
- **Electricity** (Units 10--11): electrostatics, electric fields and potential, capacitors, DC circuits, Kirchhoff's rules, RC transients
- **Magnetism** (Unit 12): magnetic fields, forces on currents and moving charges, electromagnetic induction
- **Optics** (Units 13--14): mirrors and lenses, interference, diffraction
- **Modern Physics** (Unit 15): photons, atomic models, nuclear physics, radioactive processes

## Core Skill Areas

Three skill areas are emphasized throughout the course:

1. **Visual Modeling** -- Construct diagrams, graphs, and representations to capture and communicate physical phenomena
2. **Quantitative Analysis** -- Apply algebraic methods to derive results, estimate quantities, and solve multi-step problems
3. **Inquiry and Reasoning** -- Design and describe investigations, interpret data, and build evidence-based arguments

## Prerequisites

Students should have completed AP Physics 1 or a comparable introductory physics course and should have taken or be concurrently taking pre-calculus.

## Key Differences from AP Physics 1

- AP Physics 2 covers **thermodynamics, electricity, magnetism, optics, and modern physics**; AP Physics 1 covers **mechanics and fluids**
- Both are algebra-based; AP Physics C (Mechanics / E&M) is calculus-based
- AP Physics 2 introduces **field models** (electric, magnetic, gravitational) as a unifying framework
- AP Physics 2 includes **quantum and nuclear physics**; AP Physics 1 does not
- AP Physics 2 covers **wave optics** (interference, diffraction); AP Physics 1 covers only mechanical oscillations

## Key Differences from IB Physics

- AP Physics 2 thermodynamics overlaps with IB Theme B (B.1, B.3, B.4)
- AP Physics 2 electricity/magnetism overlaps with IB Themes B and D (B.5, D.2, D.3, D.4)
- AP Physics 2 optics has significant overlap with IB Theme C (C.2, C.3, C.4, C.5) but also includes geometric optics not emphasized in IB
- AP Physics 2 modern physics overlaps with IB Theme E (E.1, E.2, E.3, E.4, E.5)
- IB covers **special relativity** (A.5) which AP Physics 2 does not
- IB includes the **greenhouse effect** (B.2) which is not in any AP Physics course

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Unit numbers 9--15 continue from AP Physics 1 (1--8); use the full number when referencing
- Many concepts build on AP Physics 1 foundations (energy, forces, conservation laws)
- Emphasize connections between electric and magnetic phenomena (electromagnetism is unified)
- RC circuits require understanding of exponential functions (not calculus, but exponential growth/decay)
- Modern physics topics are conceptual with some algebra; no quantum mechanics formalism
- The equation sheet is provided during the exam; students should know what is available vs. what must be derived
