# E.5: Fusion and Stars

> Theme E: Nuclear and Quantum Physics | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Nuclear Fusion
- Combining light nuclei to form a heavier nucleus + energy
- Requires extremely high temperatures (~$10^7$ K) to overcome electrostatic repulsion
- Energy released because products have higher binding energy per nucleon
- Examples:
  - Proton-proton chain (main sequence stars like the Sun):
    - $4^1_1H \rightarrow ^4_2He + 2^0_{+1}e + 2\nu_e + \text{energy}$ (net reaction)
  - Deuterium-tritium fusion (most promising for reactors):
    - $^2_1H + ^3_1H \rightarrow ^4_2He + ^1_0n + 17.6$ MeV

### Stellar Physics
- **Main sequence stars**: hydrogen fusing to helium in the core
  - Hydrostatic equilibrium: gravitational compression balanced by radiation pressure
  - Luminosity depends on mass: more massive → hotter → more luminous
  - Hertzsprung-Russell (HR) diagram: plots luminosity vs. surface temperature
    - Main sequence: diagonal band (most stars)
    - Red giants: upper right (cool, luminous)
    - White dwarfs: lower left (hot, dim)

### Stellar Evolution
- **Low-mass stars** (like the Sun):
  - Main sequence → red giant → planetary nebula → white dwarf
- **High-mass stars**:
  - Main sequence → red supergiant → supernova → neutron star or black hole
- Heavy elements (beyond iron) are created in supernovae
- **Chandrasekhar limit**: maximum mass of a white dwarf (~1.4 solar masses)

### Fusion Power (on Earth)
- Challenges: achieving and confining plasma at fusion temperatures
- **Magnetic confinement**: tokamak (toroidal magnetic field contains plasma)
- **Inertial confinement**: lasers compress fuel pellet to fusion conditions
- Advantages over fission: abundant fuel (deuterium from water), less radioactive waste, no meltdown risk
- Status: experimental (ITER, NIF); not yet commercially viable

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Nuclear fusion** | Combining light nuclei to form a heavier nucleus with release of energy |
| **Plasma** | State of matter where atoms are ionized; required for fusion |
| **Main sequence** | Stage where stars fuse hydrogen to helium; longest phase of stellar life |
| **Hydrostatic equilibrium** | Balance between gravitational collapse and outward radiation/thermal pressure |
| **HR diagram** | Hertzsprung-Russell diagram plotting stellar luminosity vs. temperature |
| **Supernova** | Explosive death of a massive star; produces elements heavier than iron |
| **Chandrasekhar limit** | Maximum mass (~1.4 $M_\odot$) for a white dwarf |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"The Sun burns fuel like fire"** -- The Sun undergoes nuclear fusion, not chemical combustion.
2. **"Fusion is easy because hydrogen is abundant"** -- The challenge is achieving the extreme temperatures and pressures needed to sustain fusion.
3. **"All stars end as black holes"** -- Only the most massive stars become black holes; smaller stars become white dwarfs or neutron stars.
4. **"Stars are in the same location on the HR diagram throughout their lives"** -- Stars move on the HR diagram as they evolve through different stages.
