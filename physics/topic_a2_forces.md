# A.2: Forces and Momentum

> Theme A: Space, Time and Motion | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Newton's Laws of Motion
- **First law (inertia)**: an object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force
- **Second law**: $\vec{F}_{net} = m\vec{a}$ (net force equals mass times acceleration)
- **Third law**: for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction (force pairs act on different objects)

### Types of Forces
- **Weight**: $W = mg$ (gravitational force on an object)
- **Normal force** ($F_N$): perpendicular contact force from a surface
- **Friction**: opposes relative motion
  - Static friction: $f_s \leq \mu_s F_N$
  - Kinetic friction: $f_k = \mu_k F_N$
- **Tension**: force transmitted through a string, rope, or cable
- **Air resistance / drag**: force opposing motion through a fluid
- **Spring force**: $F = -kx$ (Hooke's law)

### Free-Body Diagrams
- Represent all forces acting on a single object
- Forces drawn from the center of mass
- Net force determines acceleration

### Circular Motion (SL + HL)
- Centripetal acceleration: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2 r$
- Centripetal force: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- Angular velocity: $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = 2\pi f$
- Applications: banking, orbits, vertical circles

### Momentum
- Linear momentum: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$
- Newton's second law (momentum form): $\vec{F} = \frac{d\vec{p}}{dt}$
- Impulse: $J = F\Delta t = \Delta p$
- **Conservation of momentum**: total momentum is conserved in an isolated system
  - $m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = m_1 v_1' + m_2 v_2'$
- Types of collisions:
  - **Elastic**: kinetic energy conserved
  - **Inelastic**: kinetic energy not conserved
  - **Perfectly inelastic**: objects stick together

### Fluid Resistance and Terminal Velocity
- Drag force increases with speed
- Terminal velocity: when drag equals weight ($F_{drag} = mg$), acceleration = 0
- Qualitative treatment at SL; quantitative aspects at HL

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $F = ma$ | Newton's second law |
| $W = mg$ | Weight |
| $f = \mu F_N$ | Friction force |
| $p = mv$ | Linear momentum |

| $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ | Centripetal force |
| $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$ | Centripetal acceleration |
| $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$ | Angular velocity |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Centripetal force is a separate type of force"** -- It is the net inward force (gravity, tension, friction, etc.) causing circular motion.
2. **"A moving object must have a force in the direction of motion"** -- Newton's first law: no net force is needed for constant velocity.
3. **"Action and reaction cancel out"** -- They act on different objects and therefore do not cancel.
4. **"Heavier objects are harder to stop because they have more momentum"** -- Correct, but this is because $p = mv$, not because of some inherent resistance beyond mass.
