# B.1: Thermal Energy Transfers

> Theme B: The Particulate Nature of Matter | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Temperature and Heat
- **Temperature**: reflects the average random kinetic energy of a substance's particles; measured in kelvin (K)
- $T(K) = T(°C) + 273$
- **Thermal energy (heat)**: energy that moves between systems because of a temperature difference; SI unit: joule (J)
- Thermal energy spontaneously transfers from hotter regions to cooler ones until both reach the same temperature

### Internal Energy
- **Internal energy**: the combined kinetic energy of particles in random motion and the potential energy stored in intermolecular interactions
- A rise in temperature corresponds to an increase in the kinetic energy component
- During a phase change, it is the potential energy component that changes — temperature stays fixed

### Specific Heat Capacity
- $Q = mc\Delta T$
- $c$: specific heat capacity (J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹); the energy required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1 K
- Calorimetry: measuring heat capacity experimentally

### Specific Latent Heat
- $Q = mL$
- $L_f$: specific latent heat of fusion (solid ↔ liquid)
- $L_v$: specific latent heat of vaporization (liquid ↔ gas)
- While a phase transition is occurring, temperature holds steady; absorbed energy reshapes the potential energy landscape between particles

### Mechanisms of Thermal Energy Transfer
- **Conduction**: thermal energy propagates via successive particle collisions (dominant in solids)
  - Rate depends on: thermal conductivity, area, temperature gradient, thickness
  - $\frac{Q}{t} = kA\frac{\Delta T}{d}$ (Fourier's law)
- **Convection**: energy is carried by the bulk displacement of fluid (liquids and gases)
  - Natural convection: driven by density variations that arise from temperature differences
- **Radiation**: energy travels as electromagnetic waves (infrared)
  - Every object with a temperature above absolute zero emits thermal radiation
  - Stefan-Boltzmann law: $P = e\sigma AT^4$ (HL)
  - Black body: an ideal surface that absorbs and emits radiation with perfect efficiency ($e = 1$)
  - Wien's displacement law: $\lambda_{max} T = 2.9 \times 10^{-3}$ m·K (HL)

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Heat and temperature are the same"** -- Temperature is a measure of average kinetic energy; heat is energy transferred due to temperature difference.
2. **"Cold objects have no thermal energy"** -- All objects above 0 K have internal energy.
3. **"Metal is colder than wood at the same temperature"** -- They are the same temperature; metal feels colder because it conducts heat away faster.
4. **"During boiling, temperature increases"** -- Temperature remains constant during a phase change; energy goes into breaking intermolecular bonds.
