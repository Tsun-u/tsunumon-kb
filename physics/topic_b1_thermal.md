# B.1: Thermal Energy Transfers

> Theme B: The Particulate Nature of Matter | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Temperature and Heat
- **Temperature**: measure of average kinetic energy of particles; SI unit: kelvin (K)
- $T(K) = T(°C) + 273$
- **Thermal energy (heat)**: energy transferred due to temperature difference; SI unit: joule (J)
- Heat flows from higher to lower temperature until thermal equilibrium

### Internal Energy
- **Internal energy**: total kinetic energy (random motion) + potential energy (intermolecular forces) of all particles
- Temperature change → change in kinetic energy component
- Phase change → change in potential energy component (no temperature change)

### Specific Heat Capacity
- $Q = mc\Delta T$
- $c$: specific heat capacity (J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹); energy needed to raise 1 kg by 1 K
- Calorimetry: measuring heat capacity experimentally

### Specific Latent Heat
- $Q = mL$
- $L_f$: specific latent heat of fusion (solid ↔ liquid)
- $L_v$: specific latent heat of vaporization (liquid ↔ gas)
- During phase change: temperature remains constant; energy changes potential energy of particles

### Mechanisms of Thermal Energy Transfer
- **Conduction**: transfer through particle collisions (solids primarily)
  - Rate depends on: thermal conductivity, area, temperature gradient, thickness
  - $\frac{Q}{t} = kA\frac{\Delta T}{d}$ (Fourier's law)
- **Convection**: transfer by bulk movement of fluid (liquids and gases)
  - Natural convection: density differences due to temperature
- **Radiation**: transfer by electromagnetic waves (infrared)
  - All objects emit thermal radiation
  - Stefan-Boltzmann law: $P = e\sigma AT^4$ (HL)
  - Black body: perfect absorber and emitter ($e = 1$)
  - Wien's displacement law: $\lambda_{max} T = 2.9 \times 10^{-3}$ m·K (HL)

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Heat and temperature are the same"** -- Temperature is a measure of average kinetic energy; heat is energy transferred due to temperature difference.
2. **"Cold objects have no thermal energy"** -- All objects above 0 K have internal energy.
3. **"Metal is colder than wood at the same temperature"** -- They are the same temperature; metal feels colder because it conducts heat away faster.
4. **"During boiling, temperature increases"** -- Temperature remains constant during a phase change; energy goes into breaking intermolecular bonds.
