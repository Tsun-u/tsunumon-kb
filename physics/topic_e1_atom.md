# E.1: Structure of the Atom

> Theme E: Nuclear and Quantum Physics | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Atomic Models
- **Thomson model** (plum pudding): positive charge spread uniformly with embedded electrons
- **Rutherford model**: small, dense, positive nucleus; electrons orbit at distance
  - Evidence: alpha particle scattering experiment (Geiger-Marsden)
  - Most alpha particles passed through → atom is mostly empty space
  - Some deflected at large angles → small, massive, positive nucleus
- **Bohr model**: electrons in discrete orbits with quantized energy levels
  - Electrons can only occupy specific energy levels
  - Transitions between levels involve absorption or emission of photons

### Energy Levels
- Electrons in atoms occupy discrete energy levels (quantized)
- **Ground state**: lowest energy level
- **Excited state**: electron in a higher energy level
- Energy of emitted/absorbed photon: $E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
  - $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s (Planck's constant)
- **Emission spectra**: photons emitted when electrons transition to lower energy levels
  - Line spectra: discrete wavelengths characteristic of the element
- **Absorption spectra**: dark lines where photons of specific wavelengths are absorbed
- Hydrogen energy levels: $E_n = -\frac{13.6}{n^2}$ eV

### Nuclear Structure
- **Protons**: positive charge ($+e$), mass ≈ 1 u
- **Neutrons**: neutral, mass ≈ 1 u
- **Atomic number** ($Z$): number of protons
- **Mass number** ($A$): protons + neutrons
- **Isotopes**: same element, different number of neutrons
- Notation: $^A_Z X$ (e.g., $^{12}_6 C$)
- **Unified atomic mass unit**: $1 \text{ u} = 1.661 \times 10^{-27}$ kg = 931.5 MeV/c²

### Nuclear Forces
- **Strong nuclear force**: short-range (~1 fm), holds nucleons together
  - Overcomes electrostatic repulsion between protons
  - Acts equally on protons and neutrons
- Stability depends on the balance of strong force and electrostatic repulsion

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Electrons orbit the nucleus like planets"** -- The Bohr model is a simplification; quantum mechanics describes electron probability distributions (orbitals).
2. **"Atoms are solid spheres"** -- Atoms are mostly empty space; the nucleus is tiny compared to the atom.
3. **"All atoms of an element are identical"** -- Isotopes have different numbers of neutrons, giving different masses.
4. **"Continuous spectra and line spectra are the same"** -- Hot, dense matter produces continuous spectra; excited gases produce line spectra.
