# B.3: Gas Laws

> Theme B: The Particulate Nature of Matter | SL + HL

## Key Concepts

### Kinetic Molecular Theory
- Gas consists of many small particles in constant random motion
- Collisions between particles and with container walls are perfectly elastic
- The volume of individual particles is negligible compared to the container
- No intermolecular forces between particles (ideal gas assumption)
- Pressure results from particle collisions with container walls

### Ideal Gas Law
- $pV = nRT$ where:
  - $p$: pressure (Pa)
  - $V$: volume (m³)
  - $n$: number of moles
  - $R = 8.31$ J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹ (universal gas constant)
  - $T$: absolute temperature (K)
- Alternative form: $pV = NkT$ where $N$ = number of molecules, $k = 1.38 \times 10^{-23}$ J/K (Boltzmann constant)

### Gas Laws (Special Cases)
- **Boyle's law**: $pV = \text{constant}$ (at constant $T$) → $p_1V_1 = p_2V_2$
- **Charles's law**: $\frac{V}{T} = \text{constant}$ (at constant $p$)
- **Gay-Lussac's law**: $\frac{p}{T} = \text{constant}$ (at constant $V$)
- Combined gas law: $\frac{p_1V_1}{T_1} = \frac{p_2V_2}{T_2}$

### Molecular Kinetic Energy
- Average kinetic energy per molecule: $\bar{E_k} = \frac{3}{2}kT$
- Average kinetic energy per mole: $\bar{E_k} = \frac{3}{2}RT$
- Temperature is a measure of average kinetic energy of particles
- Root-mean-square speed: $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3kT}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$

### Real vs. Ideal Gases
- Ideal gas behavior is a model; real gases deviate at:
  - High pressures (particle volume becomes significant)
  - Low temperatures (intermolecular forces become significant)
- Real gases behave most ideally at high temperature and low pressure

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $pV = nRT$ | Ideal gas law (moles) |
| $pV = NkT$ | Ideal gas law (molecules) |
| $\bar{E_k} = \frac{3}{2}kT$ | Average KE per molecule |
| $p_1V_1 = p_2V_2$ | Boyle's law |
| $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3kT}{m}}$ | Root-mean-square speed |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Gas particles have the same speed"** -- Particles have a distribution of speeds (Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution); temperature relates to the average.
2. **"Temperature can be measured in °C in gas law equations"** -- Gas law equations require absolute temperature in kelvin.
3. **"Ideal gases exist"** -- The ideal gas is a model; real gases approximate ideal behavior under certain conditions.
4. **"Pressure is caused by gas particles pushing each other"** -- Pressure results from collisions with container walls, not between particles.
