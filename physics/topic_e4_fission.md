## Key Concepts

### Mass Defect and Binding Energy
- **Mass defect** ($\Delta m$): difference between mass of individual nucleons and mass of the nucleus
  - $\Delta m = [Zm_p + Nm_n] - m_{nucleus}$
- **Binding energy** ($E_b$): energy required to completely separate all nucleons
  - $E_b = \Delta m \cdot c^2$ (from $E = mc^2$)
- **Binding energy per nucleon**: $E_b / A$
  - Peak at iron-56 (~8.8 MeV per nucleon)
  - Heavy nuclei: fission releases energy (moves toward peak)
  - Light nuclei: fusion releases energy (moves toward peak)

### Nuclear Fission
- Splitting of a heavy nucleus into two lighter nuclei + neutrons + energy
- Induced fission: absorption of a neutron triggers the split
- Example: $^{235}_{92}U + ^1_0n \rightarrow ^{141}_{56}Ba + ^{92}_{36}Kr + 3^1_0n + \text{energy}$
- Products vary; many possible fission fragment combinations
- Energy released per fission: ~200 MeV

### Chain Reaction
- Released neutrons can cause further fissions
- **Critical mass**: minimum mass of fissile material needed to sustain a chain reaction
- **Subcritical**: chain reaction dies out (fewer neutrons available than needed)
- **Supercritical**: chain reaction accelerates (nuclear weapon)
- **Critical**: each fission causes exactly one more fission (steady power)

### Nuclear Power
- **Nuclear reactor** components:
  - **Fuel rods**: contain fissile material (enriched uranium or plutonium)
  - **Moderator**: slows neutrons to thermal energies (water, graphite, heavy water)
  - **Control rods**: absorb neutrons to regulate reaction rate (boron, cadmium)
  - **Coolant**: transfers thermal energy away from the core
  - **Shielding**: contains radiation (concrete, steel)
- **Thermal neutrons**: slow neutrons more effective at causing fission in U-235
- Advantages: low carbon emissions, high energy density, reliable baseload power
- Disadvantages: radioactive waste, accident risk, proliferation concerns

## Key Equations

| Equation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| $\Delta m = Zm_p + Nm_n - m_{nucleus}$ | Mass defect |
| $E_b = \Delta m c^2$ | Binding energy |
| $E_b/A$ | Binding energy per nucleon |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Nuclear fission is the same as an atomic bomb"** -- Controlled fission in reactors operates at critical (not supercritical) conditions; bombs are uncontrolled supercritical reactions.
2. **"Fission produces no waste"** -- Fission produces highly radioactive waste products that require long-term storage.
3. **"Any heavy nucleus can be used as fuel"** -- Only fissile isotopes (e.g., U-235, Pu-239) can sustain chain reactions.
4. **"Nuclear energy is always dangerous"** -- Risk is manageable with proper engineering; statistical safety records compare favorably to fossil fuels.
