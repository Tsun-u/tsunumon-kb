# Topic 3: Geometry and Trigonometry

> SL: 25 hours | HL: 51 hours

## SL Content

### 3.1 3D Geometry
- Volume and surface area of 3D solids (prisms, cylinders, spheres, cones, pyramids)
- Distance between two points in 3D: $d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2 + (z_2-z_1)^2}$
- Midpoint formula in 3D

### 3.2 Trigonometric Ratios
- Right-triangle trigonometry: $\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$, $\tan\theta$ (SOH-CAH-TOA)
- Exact values for special angles: 0¬į, 30¬į, 45¬į, 60¬į, 90¬į
- Pythagorean identity: $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$

### 3.3 Non-Right Triangles
- **Sine rule**: $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$
  - Ambiguous case (two possible triangles)
- **Cosine rule**: $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos C$
- **Area of triangle**: $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$

### 3.4 Radian Measure
- $\pi$ radians = 180¬į; conversion between degrees and radians
- Arc length: $s = r\theta$ (where $\theta$ in radians)
- Sector area: $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$

### 3.5 Unit Circle and Trigonometric Functions
- Defining $\sin$, $\cos$, $\tan$ using the unit circle
- Signs in each quadrant (ASTC: All, Sin, Tan, Cos)
- Periodic behavior: $\sin$ and $\cos$ have period $2\pi$; $\tan$ has period $\pi$
- Graphs of $\sin x$, $\cos x$, $\tan x$
- Transformations: amplitude, period, phase shift, vertical shift
- $f(x) = a\sin(b(x-c)) + d$

### 3.6 Trigonometric Equations
- Solving basic equations: $\sin x = k$, $\cos x = k$, $\tan x = k$
- Using identities and factoring to solve more complex equations
- Finding all solutions in a given interval

## HL Extension

### 3.7 Reciprocal and Inverse Trigonometric Functions
- $\sec\theta = 1/\cos\theta$, $\csc\theta = 1/\sin\theta$, $\cot\theta = 1/\tan\theta$
- $\arcsin$, $\arccos$, $\arctan$: domains, ranges, and graphs
- Pythagorean identities: $1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$, $1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$

### 3.8 Compound Angle Identities
- $\sin(A \pm B) = \sin A\cos B \pm \cos A\sin B$
- $\cos(A \pm B) = \cos A\cos B \mp \sin A\sin B$
- $\tan(A \pm B) = \frac{\tan A \pm \tan B}{1 \mp \tan A\tan B}$
- **Double angle formulas**:
  - $\sin 2A = 2\sin A\cos A$
  - $\cos 2A = \cos^2 A - \sin^2 A = 2\cos^2 A - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2 A$

### 3.9 Vectors (HL)
- Vectors in 2D and 3D: $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix}$
- Magnitude: $|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$
- Unit vectors: $\hat{v} = \frac{\vec{v}}{|\vec{v}|}$
- **Scalar (dot) product**: $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + a_3b_3$
- **Vector (cross) product**: $\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$; magnitude = $|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta$; direction by right-hand rule
- Lines in 3D: $\vec{r} = \vec{a} + t\vec{b}$ (parametric/vector form)
- Planes: $\vec{r} \cdot \vec{n} = d$ or $ax + by + cz = d$
- Angles between lines, between planes, and between a line and a plane

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Sine rule always gives one answer"** -- The ambiguous case can give two valid triangles.
2. **"Radians and degrees are interchangeable without conversion"** -- Formulas like $s = r\theta$ require radians.
3. **"$\sin^{-1}$ is the same as $1/\sin$"** -- $\sin^{-1}$ means $\arcsin$ (inverse); $(\sin x)^{-1} = \csc x$ (reciprocal).
4. **"Vectors and scalars can be added"** -- Vectors and scalars are different types; they cannot be directly combined.
