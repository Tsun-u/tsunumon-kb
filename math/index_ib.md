> Based on the IB Diploma Programme Mathematics: Analysis and Approaches Guide.

## Course Structure

IB Math AA is organized into **five topics**. Both SL and HL study all five topics, but HL covers them in significantly greater depth and breadth.

## Key Concepts

The course is built around the following mathematical concepts:
- **Approximation** -- rounding, estimation, bounds of accuracy
- **Change** -- rates of change, calculus, modeling dynamic systems
- **Equivalence** -- algebraic transformations, identities, proofs
- **Generalization** -- patterns, sequences, induction, abstraction
- **Modelling** -- using mathematical functions to represent real-world phenomena
- **Representation** -- graphs, tables, algebraic and geometric forms
- **Space** -- dimensions, vectors, coordinate geometry
- **Systems** -- equations, inequalities, matrices
- **Validity** -- proof, logical argument, domain restrictions

## SL vs HL: Key Differences

| Area | SL Only | HL Extension |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Number & Algebra | Sequences, series, binomial theorem, logs | Complex numbers, proof by induction, counting principles |
| Functions | Composites, transformations, quadratics | Rational functions, odd/even, factor/remainder theorem, partial fractions |
| Geometry & Trig | Sine/cosine rules, radian measure, unit circle | Vectors (3D), reciprocal/inverse trig, compound angle identities |
| Statistics | Descriptive stats, probability, binomial/normal distributions | Bayes' theorem, Poisson distribution, hypothesis testing |
| Calculus | Differentiation, integration, kinematics | Differential equations, Maclaurin series, limits, implicit differentiation |

## Key Differences from AP Mathematics

- IB Math AA is a **single integrated course** covering algebra through calculus; AP separates into Pre-Calculus, Calculus AB, Calculus BC
- IB AA HL goes **deeper in proof and abstraction** (proof by induction, complex numbers) than typical AP Calculus
- IB requires a **Mathematical Exploration** (internal assessment); AP has no equivalent
- IB Paper 1 is **non-calculator**; all AP Calculus exams allow calculators on some sections
- IB AA HL Paper 3 tests **problem-solving in unfamiliar contexts**; no AP equivalent
- AP Calculus BC covers more **series and sequences** techniques; IB HL covers more **vectors and complex numbers**
- IB uses the **GDC (Graphing Display Calculator)** extensively; AP similarly requires graphing calculators

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always distinguish SL and HL content clearly
- Use topic numbering (e.g., Topic 1.3, Topic 5.8) when referencing specific subtopics
- IB provides a **formula booklet** during exams; students should know what is given vs. what must be derived
- Emphasize conceptual understanding and multiple representations (algebraic, graphical, numerical)
- IB Math AA emphasizes **mathematical rigor and proof** more than AP; reflect this in explanations
- When discussing calculus, note that IB SL covers roughly AP Calculus AB content; IB HL extends beyond AP Calculus BC in some areas (e.g., differential equations) but covers less series content
