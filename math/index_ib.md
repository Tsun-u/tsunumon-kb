## Course Structure

IB Math AA is organized into **five topics**. Both SL and HL study all five topics, but HL covers them with significantly greater depth and breadth.

## Key Concepts

IB Math AA is built around interconnected mathematical concepts that thread through all five topics:
- **Approximation** — rounding, estimation, and bounds
- **Change** — rates of change, calculus, and dynamic systems
- **Equivalence** — algebraic transformations and identities
- **Generalization** — sequences, patterns, induction, and abstraction
- **Modelling** — representing real phenomena with mathematical functions
- **Representation** — multiple forms: graphs, tables, algebra, geometry
- **Space** — dimensions, vectors, coordinate geometry
- **Systems** — equations, inequalities, matrices
- **Validity** — logical argument, proof, and domain restrictions

## SL vs HL: Key Differences

| Area | SL Only | HL Extension |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Number & Algebra | Sequences, series, binomial theorem, logs | Complex numbers, proof by induction, counting principles |
| Functions | Composites, transformations, quadratics | Rational functions, odd/even, factor/remainder theorem, partial fractions |
| Geometry & Trig | Sine/cosine rules, radian measure, unit circle | Vectors (3D), reciprocal/inverse trig, compound angle identities |
| Statistics | Descriptive stats, probability, binomial/normal distributions | Bayes' theorem, Poisson distribution, hypothesis testing |
| Calculus | Differentiation, integration, kinematics | Differential equations, Maclaurin series, limits, implicit differentiation |

## Key Differences from AP Mathematics

- IB Math AA is a **single integrated course** spanning algebra through calculus; AP separates into distinct courses (Pre-Calculus, Calculus AB, Calculus BC)
- IB AA HL places stronger emphasis on **proof and abstraction** (induction, complex numbers) than typical AP Calculus
- IB requires a **Mathematical Exploration** as an internal assessment component; AP has no equivalent coursework requirement
- IB Paper 1 is **non-calculator**; AP Calculus allows calculators on designated sections
- IB AA HL Paper 3 presents **unfamiliar problem-solving contexts**; no AP equivalent
- AP Calculus BC goes deeper on **series and sequences** techniques; IB HL covers more **vectors and complex numbers**
- Both programmes use a **graphing calculator (GDC)** on calculator-permitted papers

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always distinguish SL and HL content clearly
- Use topic numbering (e.g., Topic 1.3, Topic 5.8) when referencing specific subtopics
- IB provides a **formula booklet** during exams; emphasize which results students must derive vs. look up
- Stress conceptual understanding and the ability to work across multiple representations (algebraic, graphical, numerical)
- IB Math AA emphasizes **mathematical rigour and proof** more than AP; reflect this in explanations
- IB SL calculus covers roughly AP Calculus AB content; IB HL extends beyond AP Calculus BC in some areas (e.g., differential equations) while covering fewer series techniques
