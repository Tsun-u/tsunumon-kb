# IB® Mathematics: Applications and Interpretation (SL/HL) — Course Framework

> IB Math AI course overview (SL and HL), covering the five-topic structure with first assessment 2021 and current through the 2028 exam sessions.
> A redeveloped AI course launches for first teaching August 2027, first assessment May 2029.
> Last updated: 2026-06-10

## Course Structure

IB Math AI is organized into **five topics**. Both SL and HL study all five topics, but HL covers them with significantly greater depth and breadth. The course emphasizes **mathematical modelling, statistics, and the use of technology** in real-world contexts.

## Key Concepts

AI shares the same conceptual lenses as AA — approximation, change, equivalence, generalization, modelling, patterns, quantity, representation, space, systems, validity — but consistently approaches them **through applied, technology-supported contexts**.

## SL vs HL: Key Differences

| Area | SL Core | HL Extension |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Number & Algebra | Scientific notation, sequences/series, financial math (compound interest, depreciation, amortization, annuities), approximation/percentage error, solving equations with technology | Laws of logarithms, infinite geometric series, complex numbers (Cartesian and polar/Euler form), **matrices** (operations, determinants, inverses, eigenvalues/eigenvectors) |
| Functions | Linear models, function basics, graphing with technology, modelling with linear/quadratic/exponential/cubic/sinusoidal/variation models | Composite/inverse functions, graph transformations, logistic and piecewise models, **log-log and semi-log linearization** |
| Geometry & Trig | 3D solids (volume/surface area), sine/cosine rules, bearings, arcs and sectors (degrees), perpendicular bisectors, **Voronoi diagrams** | Radians, unit circle, matrix transformations, **vectors** (lines, kinematics, scalar/vector products), **graph theory** (adjacency matrices, Eulerian/Hamiltonian, MST — Kruskal/Prim, Chinese postman, travelling salesman) |
| Statistics & Probability | Sampling techniques, data presentation, descriptive statistics, Pearson's r and regression, probability, binomial and normal distributions, **Spearman's rank**, **chi-square tests and t-tests** | Non-linear regression and $R^2$, linear combinations of random variables, unbiased estimators, central limit theorem, confidence intervals, **Poisson distribution**, type I/II errors and critical regions, **Markov chains** (transition matrices, steady state) |
| Calculus | Limits (informal), derivatives of polynomials, tangents/normals, optimization in context, anti-differentiation, **trapezoidal rule** | Derivatives of standard functions with chain/product/quotient rules, further integration, areas/volumes of revolution, kinematics, differential equations (separation of variables), **Euler's method**, **slope fields**, **coupled systems and phase portraits** |

## Key Differences from IB Math AA

- AI emphasizes **applied mathematics, modelling, statistics, and technology**; AA emphasizes **pure mathematics, algebraic technique, and proof**
- AI HL distinctive content with no AA equivalent: **matrices and eigenvalues, graph theory, Voronoi diagrams, Markov chains, Poisson distribution, phase portraits**
- AA distinctive content not in AI: formal proof (induction, contradiction), Maclaurin series, deeper integration techniques (by parts, advanced substitution), binomial theorem with fractional exponents
- **Every AI paper allows (and requires) technology**; AA Paper 1 is non-calculator
- AI statistics goes substantially deeper than AA at both levels; AA calculus goes deeper than AI
- AI SL calculus is lighter than AA SL (19 vs 28 hours); AI is generally chosen by students heading into social sciences, business, design, or data-rich fields

## Key Differences from AP Statistics / AP Calculus

- AI SL statistics overlaps heavily with AP Statistics topics (sampling, regression, probability distributions, chi-square and t-tests) but is integrated into a broader math course rather than standalone
- AI HL adds distributions AP Statistics does not test (Poisson) and structures (Markov chains)
- AI calculus (even HL) is narrower than AP Calculus BC — no formal limit definitions, no series convergence tests, no Taylor/Maclaurin series
- AI HL Euler's method and logistic/differential equation modelling overlap with AP Calculus BC Unit 7
- IB requires the Mathematical Exploration (IA); AP has no coursework component

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always distinguish SL and HL content clearly; HL sub-topics are labelled AHL
- Use sub-topic numbering (e.g., SL 4.11, AHL 3.16) when referencing specific content
- IB provides a **formula booklet** during exams; help students understand what is given vs. what must be derived
- AI students should use the GDC fluently — technology use is examined, not optional
- Frame concepts through real-world modelling contexts (finance, biology, logistics) rather than abstract derivations
- When discussing statistics, note that AI SL covers roughly AP Statistics breadth at lower depth, while AI HL exceeds AP Statistics in several areas (Poisson, Markov chains, formal estimators)
