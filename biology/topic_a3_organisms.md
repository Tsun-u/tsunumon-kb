# A3: Unity and Diversity of Organisms

> Theme A: Unity and Diversity | Level 3: Organisms

## Subtopics

### A3.1 Diversity of Organisms (SL + HL)

- Biodiversity at three levels: genetic diversity, species diversity, ecosystem diversity
- Binomial nomenclature: genus + species (italicized, e.g., *Homo sapiens*)
- Dichotomous keys for organism identification
- Five-kingdom classification vs. three-domain system
- Characteristics of major taxa:
  - **Bacteria**: prokaryotic, peptidoglycan cell walls
  - **Archaea**: prokaryotic, distinct membrane lipids and cell wall chemistry
  - **Eukarya**: eukaryotic (Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia)
- Plant diversity: bryophytes, filicinophytes, coniferophytes, angiospermophytes
- Animal diversity: key phyla (Porifera, Cnidaria, Mollusca, Annelida, Arthropoda, Chordata)

### A3.2 Classification and Cladistics (SL + HL, with HL extension)

- Natural classification: grouping organisms by shared evolutionary history
- **Cladistics**: classification based on shared derived characteristics
  - Cladograms: branching diagrams showing evolutionary relationships
  - Nodes represent common ancestors
  - Clades: groups including an ancestor and all its descendants
- Molecular phylogenetics: using DNA/RNA/protein sequences to determine relationships
  - rRNA base sequences for three-domain classification
- Analogous vs. homologous structures
  - Homologous: shared ancestry (e.g., pentadactyl limb)
  - Analogous: convergent evolution (e.g., wings in birds vs. insects)

#### HL Extension -- A3.2
- Constructing and interpreting detailed cladograms from molecular data
- Using multiple lines of evidence for classification
- Reclassification of organisms based on new molecular evidence

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Biodiversity** | Variety of life at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels |
| **Binomial nomenclature** | Two-part naming system: genus + species |
| **Clade** | Group of organisms including a common ancestor and all its descendants |
| **Cladogram** | Branching diagram showing evolutionary relationships based on shared derived characteristics |
| **Homologous structures** | Structures similar due to shared ancestry |
| **Analogous structures** | Structures with similar function but different evolutionary origin |
| **Taxon** | A named group of organisms at any level of classification (plural: taxa) |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Classification is fixed and permanent"** -- Classification is revised as new evidence (especially molecular) emerges.
2. **"Cladograms show how evolved an organism is"** -- Cladograms show relatedness, not degree of evolution; all living species are equally evolved.
3. **"Similarity always means close relationship"** -- Analogous structures from convergent evolution can mislead; molecular evidence is more reliable.
4. **"Archaea are just weird bacteria"** -- Archaea are a distinct domain, as different from Bacteria as they are from Eukarya.
