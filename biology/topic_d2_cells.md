# D2: Continuity and Change -- Cells

> Theme D: Continuity and Change | Level 2: Cells

## Subtopics

### D2.1 Cell and Nuclear Division (SL + HL)

- **Cell cycle**: interphase (G1, S, G2) + mitotic phase (mitosis + cytokinesis)
- **Interphase**:
  - G1: cell growth, organelle replication
  - S: DNA replication
  - G2: preparation for division, error checking
- **Mitosis**: division of the nucleus into two genetically identical daughter nuclei
  - Prophase: chromatin condenses → chromosomes; nuclear envelope breaks down; spindle forms
  - Metaphase: chromosomes align at metaphase plate
  - Anaphase: sister chromatids separate to opposite poles
  - Telophase: nuclear envelopes reform; chromosomes decondense
  - Cytokinesis: cytoplasm divides (cleavage furrow in animals; cell plate in plants)
- **Meiosis**: produces four genetically diverse haploid cells
  - Meiosis I: homologous pairs separate (reductional division)
    - Crossing over in prophase I → genetic recombination
    - Independent assortment of homologous pairs at metaphase I
  - Meiosis II: sister chromatids separate (similar to mitosis)
- Significance: meiosis produces gametes; mitosis produces somatic cells

#### HL Extension -- D2.1
- Cell cycle regulation: cyclins and CDKs
- Checkpoints (G1, G2, spindle assembly)
- Uncontrolled cell division → cancer (oncogenes, tumor suppressors)
- Nondisjunction and chromosomal abnormalities (e.g., Down syndrome: trisomy 21)

### D2.2 Gene Expression (SL + HL)

- Not all genes are expressed in every cell; **differential gene expression** creates cell diversity
- Levels of gene regulation:
  - Transcriptional: transcription factors, promoters, enhancers
  - Post-transcriptional: alternative splicing, mRNA stability
  - Translational: regulation of translation initiation
  - Post-translational: protein modification, folding, degradation
- **Epigenetics**: heritable changes in gene expression without changes to DNA sequence
  - DNA methylation: addition of methyl groups → gene silencing
  - Histone modification: acetylation/deacetylation affects chromatin structure
- Environmental influences on gene expression (e.g., temperature, nutrition)

#### HL Extension -- D2.2
- The lac operon model of gene regulation in prokaryotes
- Eukaryotic gene regulation in detail (enhancers, silencers, transcription factor complexes)
- Monozygotic twin studies and epigenetics

### D2.3 Water Potential (HL only)

- **Water potential** ($\Psi$): tendency of water to move from one area to another
- $\Psi = \Psi_s + \Psi_p$ (solute potential + pressure potential)
- Pure water has $\Psi = 0$; adding solute decreases water potential (more negative)
- Water moves from higher $\Psi$ to lower $\Psi$ (by osmosis)
- In plant cells:
  - **Turgid**: cell in hypotonic solution; $\Psi_p$ is positive (turgor pressure)
  - **Plasmolysed**: cell in hypertonic solution; membrane pulls away from cell wall
  - **Incipient plasmolysis**: $\Psi_p = 0$; cell membrane just separates from wall
- Significance for plant water uptake, transport, and stomatal function

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Mitosis** | Nuclear division producing two genetically identical daughter nuclei |
| **Meiosis** | Nuclear division producing four genetically diverse haploid cells |
| **Crossing over** | Exchange of DNA segments between homologous chromosomes during meiosis I |
| **Independent assortment** | Random orientation of homologous pairs at metaphase I |
| **Epigenetics** | Heritable changes in gene expression not caused by DNA sequence changes |
| **DNA methylation** | Addition of methyl groups to DNA, typically silencing gene expression |
| **Water potential ($\Psi$)** | Measure of the tendency of water to move from one area to another |
| **Turgor pressure** | Pressure exerted by cell contents against the cell wall |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Mitosis produces two cells"** -- Mitosis is nuclear division; cytokinesis is the division of the cytoplasm. Together they produce two cells.
2. **"Meiosis occurs throughout the body"** -- Meiosis only occurs in reproductive organs (ovaries and testes in animals).
3. **"Crossing over happens in mitosis"** -- Crossing over is specific to meiosis I (prophase I).
4. **"All genes are active in every cell"** -- Differential gene expression means each cell type activates only a subset of genes.
5. **"Epigenetic changes are mutations"** -- Epigenetic changes alter gene expression without changing the DNA sequence itself.
