# IB® Biology (SL/HL) -- Course Framework

> Covers IB Biology content as structured in the 2025 IB Diploma Programme syllabus (SL/HL).
> Last updated: 2026-06-14

## Course Structure

The IB Biology syllabus is organized around **four themes**, each viewed through **four levels of biological organization** (Molecules, Cells, Organisms, Ecosystems). This creates a 4x4 grid of 16 topic areas containing ~40 subtopics.

### Four Themes

| Code | Theme | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **A** | Unity and Diversity | Examining what all living things share and how biological variety arises across species and populations |
| **B** | Form and Function | Exploring how the shape, structure, and organization of biological components determine what they do |
| **C** | Interaction and Interdependence | Investigating how living systems communicate, cooperate, and depend on one another and their environments |
| **D** | Continuity and Change | Understanding how genetic information is preserved and passed on, and how living systems evolve over time |

### Four Levels of Organization

| Level | Code | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | Molecules | Biochemistry and molecular biology |
| 2 | Cells | Cell biology and cellular processes |
| 3 | Organisms | Physiology and whole-organism biology |
| 4 | Ecosystems | Ecology and environmental biology |

## Key Differences from AP Biology

- IB Biology uses a **thematic** organization (4 themes x 4 levels) rather than AP's **unit-based** linear structure
- IB has **HL-only subtopics** embedded within each theme, not separate units
- IB includes a mandatory **Internal Assessment** (scientific investigation)
- IB Biology covers **ecology and conservation** more explicitly at the ecosystem level
- AP Biology emphasizes **Big Ideas** (Evolution, Energetics, Information, Systems); IB uses themes (Unity/Diversity, Form/Function, Interaction, Continuity/Change)
- IB's 2025 syllabus removed option topics; content is now integrated into SL/HL pathways

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always clarify whether content is SL or HL when teaching
- Use the theme-level code (e.g., B2.1) when referencing specific content
- Distinguish clearly between similar concepts across themes (e.g., natural selection appears in both A4 and D4)
- IB emphasizes connections between topics; use linking questions to connect across themes
- The Nature of Science (NOS) is integrated throughout; highlight experimental evidence and scientific methodology
