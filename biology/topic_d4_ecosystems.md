# D4: Continuity and Change -- Ecosystems

> Theme D: Continuity and Change | Level 4: Ecosystems

## Subtopics

### D4.1 Natural Selection (SL + HL)

- **Natural selection** as the mechanism of evolution (also see A4.1)
- Requirements for natural selection:
  1. Variation exists within a population (heritable)
  2. Overproduction of offspring (more than can survive)
  3. Competition for limited resources (struggle for existence)
  4. Differential survival and reproduction (fitness)
  5. Favorable traits passed to next generation
- Types of selection:
  - **Stabilizing**: favors intermediate phenotypes (e.g., birth weight in humans)
  - **Directional**: favors one extreme (e.g., antibiotic resistance in bacteria)
  - **Disruptive**: favors both extremes (e.g., beak size in seed-eating birds)
- Examples:
  - Peppered moth (*Biston betularia*): industrial melanism
  - Antibiotic resistance in bacteria
  - Darwin's finches: adaptive radiation

#### HL Extension -- D4.1
- Sexual selection and mate choice
- Kin selection and altruism
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium: $p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1$ and $p + q = 1$
  - Conditions: large population, random mating, no mutation, no migration, no selection

### D4.2 Stability and Change in Ecosystems (SL + HL)

- Ecosystems tend toward dynamic equilibrium (stability)
- **Resilience**: ability of an ecosystem to recover after disturbance
- **Resistance**: ability of an ecosystem to withstand disturbance without change
- Factors affecting ecosystem stability:
  - Biodiversity: generally, higher biodiversity → greater stability
  - Complexity of food webs: more pathways → more resilience
  - Keystone species: their removal causes disproportionate effects
- Human impacts on ecosystems:
  - Habitat destruction, fragmentation
  - Pollution (water, air, soil)
  - Introduction of invasive species
  - Overexploitation of resources
- Ecosystem monitoring and indicators of change

### D4.3 Climate Change (SL + HL)

- **Enhanced greenhouse effect**: human activities increase greenhouse gas concentrations
  - CO2 (fossil fuel combustion, deforestation)
  - CH4 (agriculture, landfills, natural gas)
  - N2O (fertilizers, combustion)
- Evidence for climate change:
  - Rising global temperatures, melting ice caps, rising sea levels
  - Changes in precipitation patterns, ocean acidification
  - Ice core data: correlation between CO2 and temperature over millennia
- Biological impacts of climate change:
  - Shifts in species distribution (poleward and upward migration)
  - Changes in phenology (timing of events: flowering, migration, breeding)
  - Coral bleaching (loss of symbiotic zooxanthellae due to warming)
  - Increased frequency of extreme weather events
  - Threats to biodiversity: species unable to adapt or migrate fast enough
- Mitigation strategies: reducing emissions, carbon capture, renewable energy
- Adaptation strategies: changing agricultural practices, conservation planning

#### HL Extension -- D4.3
- Carbon cycle disruption in detail
- Feedback loops in climate change (positive: ice-albedo; negative: increased plant growth)
- International policy responses (Paris Agreement, IPCC)

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Stabilizing selection** | Natural selection favoring intermediate phenotypes |
| **Directional selection** | Natural selection favoring one extreme phenotype |
| **Disruptive selection** | Natural selection favoring both extreme phenotypes |
| **Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium** | Model predicting allele and genotype frequencies in a non-evolving population |
| **Ecosystem resilience** | Ability of an ecosystem to recover from disturbance |
| **Enhanced greenhouse effect** | Increase in greenhouse effect due to human-caused rises in atmospheric greenhouse gases |
| **Phenology** | Study of timing of seasonal biological events |
| **Ocean acidification** | Decrease in ocean pH due to absorption of excess atmospheric CO2 |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Natural selection acts on individuals"** -- Selection acts on individuals, but evolution occurs in populations.
2. **"Evolution is just a theory"** -- In science, "theory" means a well-supported explanation, not a guess.
3. **"Climate change is only about temperature"** -- Climate change affects precipitation, sea level, ocean chemistry, and ecosystem dynamics.
4. **"Ecosystems are fragile and cannot recover"** -- Many ecosystems show remarkable resilience, though recovery depends on the severity and type of disturbance.
5. **"All species will adapt to climate change"** -- Many species face extinction because the rate of change exceeds their capacity to adapt or migrate.
