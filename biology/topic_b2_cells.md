# B2: Form and Function of Cells

> Theme B: Form and Function | Level 2: Cells

## Subtopics

### B2.1 Membranes and Membrane Transport (SL + HL)

- **Fluid mosaic model** of membrane structure:
  - Phospholipid bilayer with embedded and peripheral proteins
  - Cholesterol: regulates membrane fluidity
  - Glycoproteins and glycolipids: cell recognition and signaling
- Membrane functions: barrier, transport, cell communication, enzymatic activity
- **Passive transport** (no energy required):
  - **Simple diffusion**: movement of nonpolar/small molecules down concentration gradient
  - **Facilitated diffusion**: movement through channel or carrier proteins down gradient
  - **Osmosis**: diffusion of water through a selectively permeable membrane
- **Active transport** (energy required):
  - Pump proteins (e.g., Na+/K+ ATPase): against concentration gradient, uses ATP
  - **Endocytosis**: phagocytosis and pinocytosis (cell engulfs material)
  - **Exocytosis**: vesicles fuse with membrane to release contents
- Hypertonic, hypotonic, isotonic solutions and their effects on cells
- Plasmolysis in plant cells; lysis in animal cells

#### HL Extension -- B2.1
- Detailed analysis of membrane protein types and functions
- Electrochemical gradients and cotransport
- Selectivity of ion channels (voltage-gated, ligand-gated)

### B2.2 Organelles and Compartmentalisation (SL + HL, with HL extension)

- Advantage of compartmentalisation: separation of metabolic processes, increased surface area for reactions
- Organelle structure and function:
  - **Nucleus**: contains DNA, nuclear envelope with pores, nucleolus (rRNA synthesis)
  - **Rough ER**: protein synthesis and modification (ribosomes on surface)
  - **Smooth ER**: lipid synthesis, detoxification
  - **Golgi apparatus**: protein modification, sorting, packaging, secretion
  - **Mitochondria**: aerobic respiration (ATP production); double membrane, cristae, matrix
  - **Chloroplasts**: photosynthesis; double membrane, thylakoids, grana, stroma
  - **Lysosomes**: intracellular digestion (hydrolytic enzymes)
  - **Vacuoles**: storage, turgor pressure (plant cells)

#### HL Extension -- B2.2
- Endomembrane system: interconnection of ER, Golgi, vesicles, and plasma membrane
- Detailed mitochondrial and chloroplast ultrastructure and function

### B2.3 Cell Specialisation (SL + HL)

- **Differentiation**: process by which unspecialised cells become specialised
- All cells in an organism contain the same genome; differential gene expression creates specialised cells
- Examples of specialised cells:
  - Red blood cells: no nucleus, biconcave shape, hemoglobin
  - Neurons: long axons, dendrites, synaptic terminals
  - Muscle cells: many mitochondria, contractile proteins (actin, myosin)
  - Root hair cells: extended surface area for water absorption
  - Guard cells: regulate stomata opening
- **Stem cells**: undifferentiated cells capable of self-renewal and differentiation
  - Embryonic stem cells: pluripotent
  - Adult stem cells: multipotent
  - Therapeutic uses and ethical considerations

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Fluid mosaic model** | Model of membrane structure: phospholipid bilayer with diverse embedded proteins |
| **Osmosis** | Net movement of water from lower solute to higher solute concentration across a semipermeable membrane |
| **Active transport** | Movement of substances against their concentration gradient, requiring ATP |
| **Compartmentalisation** | Separation of cellular processes into membrane-bound organelles |
| **Differentiation** | Process by which cells become specialised for specific functions |
| **Stem cell** | Unspecialised cell capable of dividing and differentiating into specialised cell types |
| **Plasmolysis** | Shrinkage of cell membrane away from cell wall in hypertonic solution |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"The cell membrane is rigid"** -- The membrane is fluid; phospholipids and proteins move laterally.
2. **"Osmosis only applies to water"** -- Osmosis specifically refers to water movement through a semipermeable membrane.
3. **"Active transport always uses ATP directly"** -- Some active transport uses electrochemical gradients (secondary active transport).
4. **"All cells look the same"** -- Cells are highly specialised in structure to match their function.
5. **"Stem cells are only found in embryos"** -- Adult stem cells exist in many tissues (bone marrow, skin, gut).
