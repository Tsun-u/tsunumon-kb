# C3: Interaction and Interdependence -- Organisms

> Theme C: Interaction and Interdependence | Level 3: Organisms

## Subtopics

### C3.1 Integration of Body Systems (SL + HL)

- Coordination of organ systems for homeostasis and survival
- **Digestive system**:
  - Mouth (mechanical + amylase) → stomach (pepsin, HCl) → small intestine (pancreatic enzymes, bile)
  - Absorption: villi and microvilli increase surface area in small intestine
  - Large intestine: water absorption
- **Excretory system** (HL emphasis):
  - Kidney structure: cortex, medulla, renal pelvis
  - Nephron: glomerulus → Bowman's capsule → proximal tubule → loop of Henle → distal tubule → collecting duct
  - Ultrafiltration, selective reabsorption, osmoregulation
  - ADH and water balance (feedback loop)
- **Endocrine system** integration with nervous system
  - Hypothalamus-pituitary axis as the link between nervous and endocrine systems
  - Fight-or-flight response: sympathetic nervous system + adrenaline

### C3.2 Defence Against Disease (SL + HL)

- **First line of defence**: barriers
  - Skin, mucous membranes, stomach acid, lysozyme in tears/saliva
- **Second line of defence**: non-specific innate immunity
  - Phagocytes (macrophages, neutrophils): engulf and digest pathogens
  - Inflammation: increased blood flow, swelling, redness, heat
  - Fever: elevated body temperature inhibits pathogen growth
- **Third line of defence**: specific adaptive immunity
  - **Antigens**: molecules (usually on cell surface) that trigger immune response
  - **B lymphocytes**: produce antibodies (humoral immunity)
    - Antibody structure: Y-shaped, variable region binds specific antigen
    - Plasma cells and memory cells
  - **T lymphocytes**: cell-mediated immunity
    - Helper T cells: activate B cells and other immune cells
    - Cytotoxic T cells: destroy infected cells
  - **Vaccination**: exposure to weakened/inactivated pathogen or antigen → memory cells → faster secondary response
- Antibiotics: effective against bacteria, not viruses
- Antibiotic resistance: natural selection in bacterial populations

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Pathogen** | Organism or agent that causes disease |
| **Antigen** | Molecule that triggers a specific immune response |
| **Antibody** | Protein produced by B cells that binds specifically to an antigen |
| **Phagocyte** | White blood cell that engulfs and digests pathogens and debris |
| **Vaccination** | Administration of antigenic material to stimulate immunity without causing disease |
| **Ultrafiltration** | Filtration of blood under pressure in the glomerulus of the kidney |
| **Homeostasis** | Maintenance of a stable internal environment despite external changes |
| **Osmoregulation** | Control of water and solute balance in body fluids |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Antibiotics work against viruses"** -- Antibiotics target bacteria only; antiviral drugs target viruses.
2. **"Antibodies kill pathogens directly"** -- Antibodies mark pathogens for destruction; phagocytes and complement do the killing.
3. **"Vaccination causes disease"** -- Vaccines use weakened/inactivated pathogens or fragments; they do not cause the disease.
4. **"The immune system always works perfectly"** -- Autoimmune diseases, allergies, and immunodeficiencies show immune dysfunction.
5. **"Kidneys only remove waste"** -- Kidneys also regulate water balance, blood pressure, pH, and electrolytes.
