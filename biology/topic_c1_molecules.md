# C1: Interaction and Interdependence -- Molecules

> Theme C: Interaction and Interdependence | Level 1: Molecules

## Subtopics

### C1.1 Enzymes and Metabolism (SL + HL)

- **Enzymes** as biological catalysts: proteins that speed up reactions by lowering activation energy
- **Active site**: region of enzyme that binds substrate (lock-and-key model, induced fit model)
- Enzyme-substrate complex → products released, enzyme recycled
- Factors affecting enzyme activity:
  - **Temperature**: increases rate up to optimum, then denaturation
  - **pH**: each enzyme has an optimal pH; extremes cause denaturation
  - **Substrate concentration**: rate increases until all active sites saturated ($V_{max}$)
- **Enzyme inhibition**:
  - **Competitive inhibition**: inhibitor binds to active site; overcome by increased substrate
  - **Non-competitive inhibition**: inhibitor binds elsewhere, changes active site shape; cannot be overcome by increasing substrate
- Metabolic pathways: series of enzyme-catalysed reactions (anabolic and catabolic)
- End-product inhibition (negative feedback in metabolic pathways)

#### HL Extension -- C1.1
- Michaelis-Menten kinetics: $V_{max}$, $K_m$ (substrate concentration at half $V_{max}$)
- Allosteric regulation of enzyme activity
- Cofactors and coenzymes (e.g., NAD+, FAD)

### C1.2 Cell Respiration (SL + HL)

- Cell respiration: controlled release of energy from organic compounds to produce ATP
- **Anaerobic respiration** (without oxygen):
  - **Glycolysis**: glucose → 2 pyruvate (net 2 ATP, 2 NADH); occurs in cytoplasm
  - Fermentation: pyruvate → ethanol + CO2 (yeast) or lactate (animals)
- **Aerobic respiration** (with oxygen):
  - Overall: $C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O$ (+ ~36--38 ATP)
  - **Link reaction**: pyruvate → acetyl CoA + CO2 (in mitochondrial matrix)
  - **Krebs cycle**: acetyl CoA → CO2 + NADH + FADH2 + ATP (in matrix)
  - **Oxidative phosphorylation**: electron transport chain + chemiosmosis (on inner mitochondrial membrane/cristae)
    - NADH and FADH2 donate electrons → proton gradient → ATP synthase

- Respiratory substrates other than glucose (lipids, proteins)

### C1.3 Photosynthesis (SL + HL)

- Photosynthesis: conversion of light energy to chemical energy in organic molecules
- Overall: $6CO_2 + 6H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{light}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$
- **Light-dependent reactions** (thylakoid membranes):
  - Photosystem II and Photosystem I absorb light energy
  - Photolysis of water: $2H_2O \rightarrow O_2 + 4H^+ + 4e^-$
  - Electron transport chain generates ATP (photophosphorylation) and NADPH
- **Light-independent reactions / Calvin cycle** (stroma):
  - Carbon fixation: CO2 + RuBP → 2 GP (catalysed by RuBisCO)
  - Reduction: GP → G3P (uses ATP and NADPH)
  - Regeneration of RuBP
- Limiting factors: light intensity, CO2 concentration, temperature
- Absorption and action spectra of photosynthetic pigments

- Chemiosmosis in chloroplasts (comparison with mitochondria)

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Activation energy** | Minimum energy required for a reaction to proceed |
| **Active site** | Region of an enzyme where the substrate binds |
| **Induced fit** | Model where enzyme active site changes shape slightly to accommodate substrate |
| **Glycolysis** | Breakdown of glucose to pyruvate; occurs in cytoplasm, no oxygen required |
| **Krebs cycle** | Cyclic pathway in mitochondrial matrix that oxidizes acetyl CoA to CO2 |
| **Oxidative phosphorylation** | ATP production using energy from electron transport chain and chemiosmosis |
| **Chemiosmosis** | ATP synthesis driven by proton gradient across a membrane |
| **Calvin cycle** | Light-independent reactions of photosynthesis; fixes CO2 into organic molecules |
| **RuBisCO** | Enzyme that catalyses carbon fixation in the Calvin cycle |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Enzymes are used up in reactions"** -- Enzymes are catalysts; they are recycled and reused.
2. **"Cells either do photosynthesis or respiration"** -- Plant cells perform both; respiration occurs continuously, photosynthesis only in light.
3. **"Respiration is just breathing"** -- Cellular respiration is a biochemical process in cells; breathing (ventilation) is a mechanical process.
4. **"Photosynthesis only produces glucose"** -- The Calvin cycle produces G3P, which is used to synthesize various organic molecules.
5. **"Anaerobic respiration produces no ATP"** -- Glycolysis produces a net gain of 2 ATP without oxygen.
