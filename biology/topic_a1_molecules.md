# A1: Unity and Diversity of Molecules

> Theme A: Unity and Diversity | Level 1: Molecules

## Subtopics

### A1.1 Water (SL + HL)

- Water as the medium for life: hydrogen bonding between water molecules
- Properties of water:
  - **Cohesion**: hydrogen bonds hold water molecules together (surface tension)
  - **Adhesion**: attraction between water and other polar molecules (capillary action)
  - **High specific heat capacity**: moderates temperature changes in organisms and environments
  - **High latent heat of vaporisation**: effective cooling through evaporation (sweating)
  - **Solvent properties**: polar nature dissolves ionic and polar substances
  - **Density anomaly**: ice is less dense than liquid water; insulates aquatic ecosystems
- Thermal properties of water and their biological significance
- Water as a transport medium (blood, xylem, phloem)
- Hydrophilic vs. hydrophobic interactions in biological systems

### A1.2 Nucleic Acids (SL + HL)

- **DNA** (deoxyribonucleic acid) as the molecule of heredity
  - Double helix structure: two antiparallel polynucleotide strands
  - Nucleotide structure: deoxyribose sugar + phosphate group + nitrogenous base
  - Complementary base pairing: A--T (two H-bonds), G--C (three H-bonds)
  - 5' to 3' directionality; antiparallel strands
- **RNA** (ribonucleic acid) structure and types
  - Single-stranded; ribose sugar; uracil replaces thymine
  - mRNA, tRNA, rRNA -- roles in protein synthesis
- Differences between DNA and RNA (sugar, bases, strandedness, stability)
- The **universal genetic code**: evidence for common ancestry
- **ATP** as a nucleotide derivative used in energy transfer

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Hydrogen bond** | Weak electrostatic attraction between a slightly positive H atom and a slightly negative O or N atom |
| **Nucleotide** | Monomer of nucleic acids: pentose sugar + phosphate + nitrogenous base |
| **Complementary base pairing** | A pairs with T (or U in RNA); G pairs with C |
| **Antiparallel** | Two DNA strands run in opposite 5'→3' directions |
| **Purine** | Double-ring nitrogenous base (adenine, guanine) |
| **Pyrimidine** | Single-ring nitrogenous base (cytosine, thymine, uracil) |
| **Hydrophilic** | Water-attracting; polar or charged molecules |
| **Hydrophobic** | Water-repelling; nonpolar molecules |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Water is just a simple molecule"** -- Water has extraordinary properties due to hydrogen bonding that are essential for life; its behavior is far from simple.
2. **"DNA and RNA are essentially the same"** -- They differ in sugar (deoxyribose vs. ribose), bases (thymine vs. uracil), strandedness, and stability.
3. **"All nucleotides are part of DNA or RNA"** -- ATP and NAD+ are nucleotide derivatives with energy-transfer functions.
4. **"Hydrogen bonds are strong bonds"** -- Individual hydrogen bonds are weak; their collective effect makes them biologically significant.
