# AP® Biology Course Framework

> Covers AP Biology content as structured in the Fall 2025 course framework (8 units, 4 Big Ideas, 6 Science Practices).

## Four Big Ideas

The Fall 2025 framework organizes AP Biology around four Big Ideas.

| # | Big Idea | Core Theme |
|---|----------|------------|
| **BIG IDEA 1** | Evolution | How evolutionary processes generate both the shared characteristics and the remarkable variety found across all life forms |
| **BIG IDEA 2** | Energetics | How living systems acquire energy and raw materials to support growth, reproduction, and internal stability |
| **BIG IDEA 3** | Information | How biological information is encoded, expressed, transmitted across generations, and used to coordinate life processes |
| **BIG IDEA 4** | Systems | How components at every level of life interact to produce emergent behaviors and complex biological properties |

## Science Practices

1. **Concept Explanation** - Articulate biological concepts, mechanisms, and models in clear written responses
2. **Visual Representations** - Interpret and reason from diagrams, graphs, and other visual depictions of biological phenomena
3. **Questions and Methods** - Identify appropriate research questions and design or evaluate scientific methods
4. **Data Representation** - Organize and communicate experimental observations and results in appropriate formats
5. **Quantitative Analysis** - Apply statistical reasoning and mathematical calculations to draw conclusions from biological data
6. **Scientific Argumentation** - Construct and critique evidence-based arguments about biological claims

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always use precise scientific terminology; never substitute colloquial terms
- When explaining chemical formulas, always write them correctly (e.g., H2O not H2O2 for water)
- Distinguish clearly between similar-sounding terms (see "Common Misconceptions" in each unit file)
- Each unit file contains a "Key Terminology" section with precise definitions to prevent factual errors
- Fall 2025 reorganized several units (e.g., Unit 1 splits macromolecules into Carbohydrates / Lipids / Nucleic Acids / Proteins; Unit 2 renamed "Cells"); see each unit file for the current topic list
