# AP Biology Course Framework

> Based on the College Board AP Biology Course and Exam Description (CED).
> Source: https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-biology

## Four Big Ideas

| Code | Big Idea | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **EVO** | Evolution | The process of evolution drives the diversity and unity of life |
| **ENE** | Energetics | Biological systems use energy and molecular building blocks to grow, reproduce, and maintain dynamic homeostasis |
| **IST** | Information Storage and Transmission | Living systems store, retrieve, transmit, and respond to information essential to life processes |
| **SYI** | Systems Interactions | Biological systems interact, and these systems and their interactions exhibit complex properties |

## Science Practices

1. **Concept Explanation** - Explain biological concepts, processes, and models presented in written format
2. **Visual Representations** - Analyze visual representations of biological concepts and processes
3. **Questions and Methods** - Determine scientific questions and methods
4. **Representing and Describing Data** - Represent and describe data
5. **Statistical Tests and Data Analysis** - Perform statistical tests and mathematical calculations to analyze and interpret data
6. **Argumentation** - Develop and justify scientific arguments using evidence

## Key Enduring Understandings

- **EVO-1**: Evolution is characterized by a change in the genetic makeup of a population over time and is supported by multiple lines of evidence.
- **EVO-2**: Organisms are linked by lines of descent from common ancestry.
- **EVO-3**: Life continues to evolve within a changing environment.
- **ENE-1**: The highly complex organization of living systems requires constant input of energy and the exchange of macromolecules.
- **ENE-2**: Cells have membranes that allow them to establish and maintain internal environments that are different from their external environments.
- **ENE-3**: Timing and coordination of biological mechanisms involved in growth, reproduction, and homeostasis depend on organisms responding to their environment.
- **ENE-4**: Communities and ecosystems change on the basis of interactions among populations and disruptions to the environment.
- **IST-1**: Heritable information provides for continuity of life.
- **IST-2**: Differences in the expression of genes account for some of the phenotypic differences between organisms.
- **IST-3**: Cells communicate by generating, transmitting, and receiving chemical signals.
- **IST-4**: Gene expression is regulated by environmental and developmental factors.
- **SYI-1**: Living systems are organized in a hierarchy of structural levels that interact.
- **SYI-2**: Competition and cooperation are important aspects of biological systems.
- **SYI-3**: Naturally occurring diversity among and between components within biological systems affects interactions with the environment.

## Usage Notes for AI Teaching Agent

- Always use precise scientific terminology; never substitute colloquial terms
- When explaining chemical formulas, always write them correctly (e.g., H2O not H2O2 for water)
- Distinguish clearly between similar-sounding terms (see "Common Misconceptions" in each unit file)
- Reference specific learning objective codes (e.g., SYI-1.A) when covering content
- Each unit file contains a "Key Terminology" section with precise definitions to prevent factual errors
