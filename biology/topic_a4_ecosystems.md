# A4: Unity and Diversity of Ecosystems

> Theme A: Unity and Diversity | Level 4: Ecosystems

## Subtopics

### A4.1 Evolution and Speciation (SL + HL)

- **Evolution**: change in the heritable characteristics of a population over successive generations
- Evidence for evolution:
  - Fossil record, comparative anatomy (homologous structures), molecular biology (DNA/protein comparisons)
  - Biogeography, observed examples of natural selection
- Mechanisms of evolution:
  - **Natural selection**: differential survival and reproduction based on heritable variation
  - **Genetic drift**: random changes in allele frequencies (more significant in small populations)
  - **Gene flow**: movement of alleles between populations through migration
- **Speciation**: formation of new species
  - **Allopatric speciation**: geographic isolation → genetic divergence → reproductive isolation
  - **Sympatric speciation**: speciation without geographic isolation (e.g., polyploidy in plants)
- Reproductive isolation mechanisms: prezygotic and postzygotic barriers
- Adaptive radiation: diversification from a common ancestor into many forms

#### HL Extension -- A4.1
- Gradualism vs. punctuated equilibrium
- Types of natural selection: directional, stabilizing, disruptive
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium and conditions for no evolution

### A4.2 Conservation of Biodiversity (SL + HL)

- Threats to biodiversity: habitat loss, overexploitation, invasive species, pollution, climate change
- In situ conservation: national parks, marine reserves, wildlife corridors
- Ex situ conservation: zoos, seed banks, captive breeding programs
- International agreements: CITES, Convention on Biological Diversity
- Simpson's reciprocal index of diversity: $D = \frac{N(N-1)}{\sum n(n-1)}$
- Ecosystem services: provisioning, regulating, cultural, supporting
- Sustainability and the balance between conservation and human development

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Natural selection** | Differential survival and reproduction due to heritable variation in traits |
| **Speciation** | Formation of a new species through evolutionary processes |
| **Allopatric speciation** | Speciation due to geographic isolation of populations |
| **Sympatric speciation** | Speciation within a single geographic area |
| **Adaptive radiation** | Rapid diversification of a lineage into many species filling different ecological niches |
| **Genetic drift** | Random change in allele frequency, most significant in small populations |
| **In situ conservation** | Conservation of species in their natural habitat |
| **Ex situ conservation** | Conservation of species outside their natural habitat |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Evolution has a goal or direction"** -- Evolution is not goal-directed; it results from selection pressures acting on random variation.
2. **"Individuals evolve"** -- Populations evolve; individuals are selected for or against.
3. **"Survival of the fittest means the strongest"** -- "Fittest" means best adapted to the current environment, not physically strongest.
4. **"Speciation requires millions of years"** -- Speciation can occur relatively rapidly, especially through polyploidy in plants.
5. **"Conservation is only about saving animals"** -- Conservation includes habitats, genetic diversity, and ecosystem services.
