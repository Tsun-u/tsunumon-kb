# B1: Form and Function of Molecules

> Theme B: Form and Function | Level 1: Molecules

## Subtopics

### B1.1 Carbohydrates and Lipids (SL + HL)

#### Carbohydrates
- General formula: $C_n(H_2O)_n$
- **Monosaccharides**: glucose (alpha and beta forms), fructose, galactose, ribose
  - Ring structure of glucose; alpha-glucose vs. beta-glucose (orientation of --OH on C1)
- **Disaccharides**: formed by condensation reactions (glycosidic bonds)
  - Maltose (glucose + glucose), sucrose (glucose + fructose), lactose (glucose + galactose)
- **Polysaccharides**: polymers of monosaccharides
  - **Starch** (amylose + amylopectin): energy storage in plants; alpha-1,4 and alpha-1,6 linkages
  - **Glycogen**: energy storage in animals; highly branched
  - **Cellulose**: structural in plant cell walls; beta-1,4 linkages; straight chains with hydrogen bonding

#### Lipids
- **Triglycerides**: glycerol + 3 fatty acids (ester bonds via condensation)
  - Saturated vs. unsaturated fatty acids; cis vs. trans double bonds
  - Functions: energy storage (more energy per gram than carbohydrates), insulation, protection
- **Phospholipids**: glycerol + 2 fatty acids + phosphate group
  - Amphipathic: hydrophilic head, hydrophobic tails
  - Basis of cell membrane bilayer structure
- **Steroids**: four fused carbon rings (e.g., cholesterol, testosterone, estrogen)

### B1.2 Proteins (SL + HL)

- **Amino acids**: central carbon bonded to amino group, carboxyl group, H, and R group
  - 20 different R groups → 20 amino acids
- **Peptide bonds**: formed by condensation between amino and carboxyl groups
- Levels of protein structure:
  - **Primary**: sequence of amino acids in polypeptide chain
  - **Secondary**: alpha-helices and beta-pleated sheets (hydrogen bonds between backbone atoms)
  - **Tertiary**: 3D folding of single polypeptide (R-group interactions: H-bonds, ionic, disulfide, hydrophobic)
  - **Quaternary**: multiple polypeptide subunits (e.g., hemoglobin has 4 subunits)
- **Denaturation**: loss of 3D structure due to changes in pH, temperature, or chemical environment
- Protein functions: enzymes, structural (collagen, keratin), transport (hemoglobin), defense (antibodies), signaling (hormones, receptors)
- **Proteome**: the complete set of proteins expressed by an organism

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Condensation reaction** | Reaction joining two molecules with the release of water |
| **Hydrolysis** | Reaction breaking a bond by adding water |
| **Glycosidic bond** | Covalent bond between monosaccharides |
| **Peptide bond** | Covalent bond between amino acids |
| **Ester bond** | Bond between fatty acid and glycerol in lipids |
| **Denaturation** | Irreversible loss of protein's 3D structure and function |
| **Amphipathic** | Having both hydrophilic and hydrophobic regions |
| **Proteome** | Complete set of proteins expressed by a cell or organism |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"All fats are bad"** -- Unsaturated fats are essential for health; phospholipids are vital for cell membranes.
2. **"Denaturation = destruction of protein"** -- Denaturation disrupts 3D structure but does not break peptide bonds (primary structure remains).
3. **"Starch and cellulose are the same because both are glucose polymers"** -- Alpha vs. beta linkages create fundamentally different structures and functions.
4. **"Protein shape doesn't matter"** -- Function depends entirely on 3D shape; even small changes can abolish function.
